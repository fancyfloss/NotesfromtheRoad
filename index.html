🌉 The Bay — By Those Who Live Here

A Local's Guide San Francisco & Beyond

Food. Nature. Community. Flavor. From the fog-drenched streets of the city to the Pacific's edge — this is the Bay for those who actually know it.

This is not a bucket list. It's not TripAdvisor. It's the Bay Area the way locals move through it — unhurried, curious, perpetually hungry, and always looking for the next hidden corner. Pick what calls to you, skip what doesn't, and make it yours.

Mix and match. Linger. Drive with the windows down. Eat something you can't pronounce. That's the whole vibe.

01 — San Francisco

Hidden Gems &
Hole-in-the-Wall

🥢

Seafood Counter · Nob Hill

Swan Oyster Depot

1517 Polk St

A 19-stool counter, cash only, lines out the door by 9am. Order the crab back with sourdough and drown yourself in briny, cold perfection. This is the one. A SF institution since 1912.

iconic locals swear by it arrive early

Old School Diner · North Beach

Sam's Diner

North Beach

Open 'til 3am. A no-frills cheeseburger-and-beer situation. The kind of place you end up at after a long night and leave feeling like everything is going to be okay. Classic late-night SF.

affordable late night old school

Sushi Counter · Hayes Valley

Sebo

517 Hayes St

Serious, seasonal Japanese fish sourced with obsessive care. Counter seating, omakase vibes on a budget. One of those quiet places that chefs eat at on their days off.

chef's pick underrated gem

Classic Diner · Embarcadero

Red's Java House

Pier 30, The Embarcadero

Cheeseburger on sourdough. Bay views. Rickety stools. Cheap. This is what SF was before it got expensive. A beloved waterfront dive that's been here since the 1930s.

super affordable true locals spot

BBQ + Bar · Divisadero

4505 Burgers & BBQ

705 Divisadero St

Brisket, jalapeño cheddar links, chicharrones, pozole — smoked low and slow. The Presidential Platter is a commitment. Come hungry, bring friends, wear stretchy pants.

BBQ shrine group-friendly

Waterfront Bar + Food · Embarcadero

Pier 23 Cafe

Pier 23, Embarcadero

Cold drinks, bay views, live music on weekends. Not Fisherman's Wharf tourist chaos — actual neighborhood energy. Sunday brunch on the dock with a bloody mary is unmatched.

local hangout live music

Prime Rib Temple · Van Ness

House of Prime Rib

1906 Van Ness Ave

English-cut prime rib carved tableside from a silver cart. Jacket required (well, almost). One of the most gloriously anachronistic dining experiences in the city. The spinning salad bowl alone is worth it.

old SF glamour dress up a little

Tiki Bar · Fairmont Hotel

The Tonga Room

950 Mason St, Nob Hill

A full indoor lagoon, indoor thunderstorms every 30 minutes, mai tais the size of your head. One of the oldest tiki bars in America. Absolutely delirious. Perfect for a SF WTF moment.

SF classic absolutely unhinged Bourdain-approved

Chinese-American Dive · Chinatown

Li Po Lounge

916 Grant Ave, Chinatown

Dark, lantern-lit, booths of red vinyl, Chinese mai tais that'll put you sideways. Named after a Tang dynasty poet. One of the most atmospheric old-school bars in SF — feels like 1952.

neighborhood character dive bar royalty

Ice Cream · Outer Sunset

Polly Ann Ice Cream

3138 Noriega St

Taro, ube, durian, lychee, red bean — flavors that'll make you forget about vanilla forever. A SF Asian community institution. Spin the wheel of flavors. Take the risk.

neighborhood legend cheap eats Asian flavors

Farmers Market · Embarcadero

Ferry Plaza Farmers Market

One Ferry Building, Tue/Thu/Sat

Biodynamic produce, fresh tamales, artisan cheese, Blue Bottle coffee, oyster bars. Tuesday mornings are the locals' secret — smaller crowd, just as good. Saturday is the full spectacle.

Sat + Tue + Thu local ritual

Korean Home Cooking · Richmond

To Hyang

Inner Richmond

A tiny, warm Korean spot that feels like eating at someone's grandmother's house. Incredible banchan, kimchi made on-site, fish head stew that requires zero Instagram documentation — just devotion.

chef's secret word of mouth

02 — The Full Flavor

Asian Diaspora

🍜
The Bay Area has one of the most vibrant, layered Asian food cultures in America. Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Filipino — often on the same block, often family-run, always real.

Vietnamese · Tenderloin / Richmond

Turtle Tower

631 Larkin St & 6th Ave location

Northern-style pho. Crystal-clear broth, silky noodles, none of the hoisin-overload. This is pho without compromise. The Tenderloin location is the original; come early or wait.

pho purist Vietnamese community pick

Vietnamese · Mission

Thanh Long

Outer Sunset

Famous for roasted Dungeness crab and garlic noodles — a SF Vietnamese signature. Been here since 1971. A family institution. The noodles alone are worth the drive to the Outer Sunset.

SF legend dungie + garlic noodles

Vietnamese · Daly City

PPQ Dungeness Island

Daly City

A Daly City Vietnamese institution. Roasted crab, fragrant lemongrass, cold beer. Come with a large group, order more food than you think you need, and be happy about it.

Daly City pride family-style

Chinese Seafood · Richmond

R&G Lounge

Chinatown / Lower Nob Hill

Salt and pepper crab, hand-pulled noodles, sizzling platters. A Chinatown cornerstone with Cantonese cooking that feels like a proper family feast. Two floors of controlled delicious chaos.

salt & pepper crab community staple

Ramen · Stanford / Palo Alto

Ramen Nagi

Stanford Shopping Center, Palo Alto

The legendary Japanese ramen chain with a Bay Area outpost right at Stanford. Rich tonkotsu, customizable spice levels, and a line that tells you everything you need to know before you even taste it.

ramen pilgrimage stanford area

Japanese · Japantown SF

Japantown SF

Post St & Buchanan, Western Addition

The largest Japantown outside Japan. Wander the malls, find mochi donuts, miso ramen, sushi burritos, Japanese curry, and row upon row of snacks you'll regret not buying more of.

neighborhood treasure explore + eat

Indian Fusion · Oakland

Juhu Beach Club Cookbook Legacy

Oakland — closed but the spirit lives

Bourdain ate here on Parts Unknown. Manchurian cauliflower, bhelpuri in a mason jar, bold Indian street food with Bay Area seasonality. It's closed now, but it shaped Oakland's food scene forever.

historic Bourdain stop

Filipino-Japanese Fusion · Union Square

Ox + Tiger

Union Square, SF

Tasting-menu magic where Japanese and Filipino flavors collide — in the best possible way. Inventive, seasonal, and deeply personal to its chefs. A splurge worth every bite.

boundary-pushing tasting menu

03 — Representing

Filipino Flavors

🇵🇭
From turo-turo counters in Daly City to elevated sisig in SoMa — Filipino food in the Bay is everywhere, layered, alive, and proud.

Elevated Filipino · Fisherman's Wharf

ABACÁ

Kimpton Alton Hotel, Fisherman's Wharf

The flagship for Filipino fine dining in SF. Elevated sisig, crispy chicken skin with spicy vinegar, ube colada, calamansi daiquiri. The menu refreshes constantly — every visit is different.

must-visit upscale Filipino

Filipino-Mexican Fusion · Mission

Señor Sisig

990 Valencia St + Ferry Building + Oakland

A Bay Area original — sisig burritos, sisig bowls, sisig tacos, sisig fries. Filipino-Mexican fusion that shouldn't work but absolutely does. Multiple locations. Affordable. Always fire.

affordable Bay Area born iconic fusion

Filipino Brunch · Mission

Alnico

1050 Valencia St, Mission

Filipino-American brunch with ube hotcakes, niloco mo ako plates, and sweet-savory everything. Photo-ready and soul-satisfying. Perfect for a lazy Sunday with too much coffee.

brunch goals Filipino-American soul

Turo-Turo · Mission District

Irma's Pampanga

Mission District, SF

Counter-service, point-and-eat Filipino comfort food. Chicken adobo that tastes like someone's tita made it. Affordable, generous portions, zero pretension. The definition of Pinoy hospitality.

super affordable no frills, all flavor

Filipino Homestyle · Excelsior

Kusina Ni Tess

Excelsior District, SF

Chicken arroz caldo, liempo, fresh lumpia, food-behind-the-glass ordering. Warm, counter-service Filipino cooking that feels exactly like home. Excelsior is SF's unsung Filipino food corridor.

community loved wallet-friendly

Silog Specialist · Daly City

Tselogs

Daly City

Tapsilog, spamsilog, lechon kare kare — and the full -silog lineup done right. Daly City is practically a Manila suburb and this spot embodies that completely. Garlic rice mandatory.

Daly City gem silog everything

Filipino Bakery · Bay Area

Red Ribbon + Goldilocks

Multiple Daly City / South Bay locations

Ube cake, ensaymada, bibingka, pan de sal — the bakery chains that Filipinos everywhere pine for when they're far from home. Pick up a whole cake and share it. No arguments about this.

Filipino bakery classics bakala to the core

FOB Kitchen · Oakland / Temescal

FOB Kitchen

Temescal, Oakland

Excellent Filipino brunch in Oakland's coolest neighborhood. Silog plates, crispy pork, shrimp chips. Temescal has great walkability — eat here, then explore the strip.

brunch game Oakland Filipino pride

04 — La Misión y Más

Latin Scene

🌮

Taquerias · Mission District

La Taqueria

2889 Mission St

No rice in the burrito — that's the hill they die on, and it's the correct hill. Named America's best burrito by various publications. The carne asada is simple and devastating. Cash only, line always worth it.

burrito royalty $10–15 Mission classic

Mission Taqueria

El Farolito

2779 Mission St

The late-night warrior. Open until 2:30am. Burritos the size of a small child. Super burrito with al pastor — the calling card. This is the Mission at its most essential.

late night cheap after-hours classic

Mexican Street Tacos · Mission

El Rancho Taqueria

Mission District

Chorizo, cabeza, chicharron tacos that feel like an Oakland taco truck stopped and decided to stay. Double corn tortilla, fresh salsa, wedge of lime. That's it. That's the whole thing.

$3-5 tacos street food energy

Colombian / Latin · Mission

Limon Rotisserie

Mission District

Peruvian rotisserie chicken, ceviche, aji verde sauce that you'll want to put on everything. Casual, bright, and wildly affordable for how good it is. The Peruvian-American dream.

Peruvian-Californian value pick

Mexican Sit-Down · Noe Valley

La Corneta Taqueria

Noe Valley / Glen Park

Fresh ingredients, a step above the average taqueria in presentation but not in pretension. Neighborhood gem that doesn't need a line to prove it's great. Family-friendly, filling, reliable.

neighborhood pick family-friendly

Oakland Oaxacan · Fruitvale

Fruitvale Tacos + Street Food

International Blvd, Oakland

Oakland's Fruitvale is arguably the most authentic Latin food corridor in the whole Bay. Weekend birria, weekend carnitas, weekend everything. Walk the strip and eat everywhere that smells right.

Oakland street food Fruitvale corridor weekend birria

05 — Pastry & Pour

Bakeries & Dim Sum

🥮

Chinese Bakery · Chinatown

Golden Gate Bakery

1029 Grant Ave, Chinatown

The egg tart. The egg tart. THE EGG TART. People wait 45 minutes. People cry. Closes randomly whenever they run out. Worth every single second. This is a SF pilgrimage.

legendary egg tart shrine $2 each

Sourdough Bakery · Various

Tartine Bakery

600 Guerrero St, Mission + Outer Sunset

Country sourdough that comes out at 5pm and sells out by 5:17pm. Croque monsieur at lunch. The morning bun with orange zest and cinnamon is a spiritual experience. Get there. Now.

SF bakery icon arrive early

Dim Sum · Richmond District

Yank Sing

101 Spear St, Financial District

Premium dim sum, cart service, impeccably fresh. The Shanghai soup dumplings (xiao long bao) alone will make you reorganize your priorities. Saturday brunch is a full occasion — dress accordingly.

splurge-worthy weekend brunch

Dim Sum · Millbrae / Richmond

Koi Palace

Daly City / Millbrae

Upscale Chinese seafood and dim sum. The live tanks are a spectacle. Families arrive in force on weekends. Millbrae location has a massive dining room that somehow always has a wait.

Chinese community go-to big-group dim sum

Pastry · Pacific Heights / Marina

b. Patisserie

2821 California St, Pacific Heights

Kouign-amann, croissants that crackle, seasonal tarts. French technique, California ingredients, Pacific Heights address. Small counter, big reputation. A ritual stop before a Presidio walk.

best pastries in SF Pacific Heights

Vietnamese Bakery · Various

Ba Le Bakery

Tenderloin + Daly City

Bánh mì that costs $4 and tastes like a $20 sandwich. Crusty French rolls, fresh jalapeño and daikon, pâté or lemongrass chicken. A daily SF ritual for those in the know.

$4 banh mi daytime staple

06 — The Bay & Beyond

Restaurants by the Water

⚓

Seafood · Tiburon, Marin

Sam's Anchor Cafe Ferry-accessible

27 Main St, Tiburon

A Marin Bay institution since 1920. Views of Angel Island, Alcatraz, and the SF skyline from an iconic waterfront deck. Take the ferry from SF — it's the move. Dog-friendly, live music on weekends, the full Bay experience.

best outdoor dining Marin Bay institution take the ferry

Farm-to-Table · Tiburon, Marin

Malibu Farm Tiburon New Location

9 Main St, Tiburon

Helene Henderson's farm-to-table California cooking with a stunning SF skyline backdrop. Vegetable-forward, locally sourced, the NorCal version of the famous Malibu Pier original. Dog-friendly patio. Weekend brunch is the move.

farm-to-table views Tiburon waterfront paired with Sam's

Legendary Seafood · Half Moon Bay

Sam's Chowder House

4210 N Cabrillo Hwy, Half Moon Bay

Clam chowder bread bowls directly on the Pacific. Fire pits on the deck, lobster rolls, raw bar, crashing waves in the background. The Half Moon Bay version of a perfect coastal meal.

Half Moon Bay icon chowder + ocean views

Mediterranean Waterfront · Berkeley

Skates on the Bay

100 Seawall Dr, Berkeley Marina

Right on the water with Golden Gate views. Dungeness crab, grilled seafood, and the kind of sunset that makes you forget everything you were stressed about. Berkeley's best kept waterfront secret.

Berkeley gem sunset dinner

Oyster Bar · Marshall, Marin

Hog Island Oyster Co.

20215 Shoreline Hwy, Marshall (+ Ferry Building)

Sit outside and shuck your own oysters with Tomales Bay as your backdrop. This is the Pacific Coast in a single experience. The farm is right there. The oysters don't get more local.

oyster pilgrimage farm-direct weekend drive

Surf Town Eats · Pacifica

Nick's Restaurant Pacifica

100 Rockaway Beach Ave, Pacifica

Perched above Rockaway Beach with full Pacific views. Seafood, weekend brunch, and the kind of bleached-wood coastal vibe that makes every dish taste better. One of Pacifica's original waterfront gems.

Pacifica locals spot Pacific views

07 — The Streets

SF Neighborhoods

🏘️

Old Money + Great Eats

Pacific Heights

Stately Victorians, b. Patisserie for morning pastry, Fillmore jazz corridor for nightlife, farmers market on Saturdays. Walk Fillmore St from California to Jackson — this is curated SF at its most livable.

Fillmore StSaturday Farmers Market

Japanese Culture Pocket

Japantown

The Japan Center malls have ramen shops, mochi donut counters, bookstores, sake bars, and row upon row of beautiful stuff. During cherry blossom season it turns into something else entirely. Don't skip the Peace Pagoda.

Japan Centercherry blossom fest (spring)

Presidio Heights

Presidio Heights & The Presidio

Walk into the Presidio National Park, see the GGB from the batteries, hike Crissy Field, then come back out to Sacramento St for coffee at Réveille or a late lunch at the Arguello Presidio Market.

GGB viewsCrissy Field walk

SF's Living Room

The Mission District

Always sunny (literally — the Mission gets more sun than anywhere else in SF). Murals on every block, Mission Dolores Park for lounging, Valencia St for bookshops and cocktail bars, 24th St for carnitas and bakeries.

Dolores ParkValencia St corridor

Creative, Gritty, Beloved

The Haight & Cole Valley

Lower Haight for dive bars and vintage shops; Upper Haight for head shops and Buena Vista Park; Cole Valley for the quieter, neighborhood-y version. Cha Cha Cha tapas on Haight is a must-do dinner.

Cha Cha Chavintage shops

Fog + Surf Culture

Outer Sunset & Ocean Beach

Fog-shrouded avenues, excellent ramen, surfers with boards tied to bikes, Ocean Beach fire pits at sunset. Thanh Long for Vietnamese crab, San Tung for dry fried chicken wings, then walk to the beach. This is real SF.

local neighborhoodOcean Beach fires

08 — Go Outside

Marin Trails, Sea Creatures & Wild Coasts

🦭

Iconic Hike · Marin

Mt. Tamalpais State Park

Mill Valley, Marin County

The mother mountain of Bay Area hiking. On a clear day you see the Pacific, the Bay, and half of Northern California. Multiple trail levels — Pantoll to East Peak is the classic. Come for sunrise or sunset.

panoramic viewsMarin crown jewel

Sea Lions + History · SF

Point Bonita Lighthouse

Marin Headlands, GGNRA

Jaw-dropping suspension bridge walk to a lighthouse perched at the Pacific's edge. Spot harbor seals and sea birds. Best on weekday mornings. The Marin Headlands trail network is entirely underrated.

hidden Marin gemseals & seabirds

Sea Creatures · Marin

Point Reyes National Seashore

Point Reyes Station

Elephant seals at Chimney Rock (December–March is peak season), tule elk on the trails, gray whales offshore in winter. Wildcat Camp backcountry or just a day hike to the Lighthouse. One of America's great coastal parks.

elephant sealswhale watchingday trip

Sea Lions · SF

Marine Mammal Center + Pier 39 Sea Lions

Sausalito + Pier 39, SF

The Marine Mammal Center in Sausalito rehabilitates seals and sea lions — tours are fascinating. Pier 39's sea lion colony arrived after the 1989 earthquake and never left. Both are joyful, loud, and free.

free to visitSF icon

Otters + Tide Pools · Monterey

17-Mile Drive + Monterey Bay Aquarium

Carmel / Pacific Grove

Sea otters float in the kelp forests of Monterey Bay — you'll see them from the Aquarium's deck or kayaking the bay. The 17-Mile Drive through Pebble Beach is a masterpiece. Carmel Beach at sunset is world-class.

sea ottersCarmel Beachday trip from SF

Underrated Marin Hike

Tennessee Valley Trail

Mill Valley, Marin Headlands

An easy-to-moderate coastal trail that ends at a secluded Pacific cove. Wild horses graze on the hillsides. Hawks ride the thermals above. The valley feels like it belongs in another century. Weekday mornings for solitude.

Marin hidden gemeasy-moderate

Sonoma Coast Underrated

Sonoma Coast State Park

Bodega Bay to Jenner

Arguably more dramatic than Big Sur and a fraction of the crowds. Goat Rock Beach (river otters and harbor seals at the mouth of the Russian River), Shell Beach tide pools, Bodega Head for whale watching. Pull over constantly.

Sonoma coast secretriver otters + sealsunderrated

Sonoma Gem

Duncans Mills + Cazadero

Russian River area, Sonoma County

A tiny gold rush town on the Russian River with antique shops, a diner, and silence. Keep driving up to Cazadero for redwood groves so dense it feels like another planet. Bay Area best-kept secret.

no touristsredwoods

09 — Hwy 1 South

Coastal Drive

🌊

Beach Town · 30 min from SF

Pacifica + Rockaway Beach

The first beach town south of SF and the most underrated. Linda Mar Beach is excellent for surfing or watching surfers. Grab clam chowder at Nick's on the bluff. The fog lifts by noon most days.

SFers' beachfree parking

Coastal Town · 45 min

Half Moon Bay

Pumpkin patches in October, surfing year-round, gorgeous beaches (Francis Beach for fires), Sam's Chowder House for dungeness crab. Mavericks surf break is offshore — in winter, the biggest waves in the world arrive here.

Mavericks in winterfire pits at Francis Beach

Tiny Town · 1.5 hrs

Pescadero + Pigeon Point

Duarte's Tavern in Pescadero for artichoke soup and a burger — one of the oldest restaurants on the CA coast. Drive 10 minutes to Pigeon Point Lighthouse and climb the bluffs. Año Nuevo State Reserve (elephant seals!) is nearby.

Duarte's TavernAño Nuevo seals

Classic · 2 hrs

Santa Cruz

Boardwalk, surf culture, university town energy. Soquel Ave for restaurants, the Westside for surf breaks, Natural Bridges for monarch butterflies (November). Anthony's for pizza, Penny Ice Creamery for dessert.

surf + beachlocal SC picks

10 — Silicon Valley Flavors

South Bay Indian Food

🫓
The South Bay has one of the best Indian food corridors outside of India itself. Fremont, Sunnyvale, and Milpitas are the holy trinity.

South Indian · Sunnyvale

Dosa by Dosa

Sunnyvale

Paper dosa, masala dosa, uttapam, sambar that's been simmering since this morning. South Indian street food perfected in the South Bay. The coconut chutney is mandatory.

incredibly affordable South Bay staple

Biryani Specialist · Fremont

Dum India Kitchen

Fremont

Slow-cooked dum biryani in sealed pots, layers of saffron rice and spiced meat, opened tableside. Fremont's Little India on Fremont Blvd is an entire food world — walk it before or after eating.

biryani obsession Fremont India corridor

North Indian · Milpitas

Chaat Bhavan

Milpitas / Fremont

Pani puri, sev puri, bhel puri, dahi puri — Indian street food in the truest form. The kind of place where the tables are plastic and the food is extraordinary. Pure Milpitas gold.

$5–10 plates chaat heaven

Thali Specialist · Sunnyvale

Udupi Palace

Sunnyvale / Fremont

All-vegetarian South Indian thali — tiny steel bowls of dal, sabzi, rice, sambar, and rasam rotating endlessly until you signal stop. Cheap, warm, and a window into the vegetarian heart of Indian cooking.

thali value South Indian veg

11 — What's Happening

Local Events & Culture

🎪

Monthly · SF / Gallery Row

First Thursday SF

Various galleries, SF / Dogpatch / SOMA

Gallery openings, art walks, studios open late, street vendors, community energy. Usually on the first Thursday of each month. Check 49 Geary and Dogpatch neighborhood listings for current programming.

monthly art walkSF creative scene

Monthly · Oakland

First Friday Oakland

Telegraph Ave, Uptown Oakland

Oakland's most electric regular event. Street art, food trucks, live music, DJs, vendors. Telegraph Ave shuts down and becomes a neighborhood block party. The best possible introduction to Oakland culture.

must-experienceOakland pride

Saturday / Weekly · Embarcadero

Ferry Plaza Farmers Market

One Ferry Building

The big one. Sixty-plus vendors, artisan cheese, oyster bars, hot tamales, biodynamic produce, Blue Bottle coffee, perfect sourdough. Tuesday and Thursday are the quieter locals' editions. Saturday is the full pageant.

Sat/Tue/ThuSF ritual

Baseball · China Basin

SF Giants @ Oracle Park

24 Willie Mays Plaza, SF

One of the most beautiful ballparks in America. McCovey Cove in right field, bay views, garlic fries, and splash hits. Get a bleacher seat with a beer and a garlic fry. American summer in its purest form.

ballpark magicgarlic fries mandatory

Basketball · SF

Golden State Warriors @ Chase Center

Chase Center, Mission Bay

The Bay's NBA team in a state-of-the-art arena in Mission Bay. Pre-game energy on Thrive City outside. Even when the team's rebuilding, the atmosphere is a Bay Area event worth attending.

NBA experienceBay Area pride

Annual · Spring

Japantown Cherry Blossom Festival

Post St, Japantown — typically April

One of the largest cherry blossom celebrations outside Japan. Taiko drummers, food stalls, traditional dance, mochi ice cream. Spring in Japantown is a genuinely beautiful cultural moment.

annual spring eventcommunity tradition

Annual · May

Carnaval SF

Harrison & 24th, Mission District

The Mission's Latin street festival — samba, salsa, Caribbean food, elaborate costumes, block after block of pure color and rhythm. Memorial Day weekend. Free. Absolutely worth the crowd.

SF Latin culturefree

12 — Go the Distance

Road Trip:
SLO + Pismo Beach

🚗
About 3.5–4 hours south on Hwy 101 or Hwy 1. The farthest and most worth-it stretch. Take the coast through Big Sur if you can — it adds time but removes stress from your entire body.

Must-Stop · Big Sur

Big Sur Coastline

The Bixby Bridge photo is mandatory but stop at McWay Falls (Julia Pfeiffer Burns SP), Nepenthe restaurant for a burger with a cliff-edge view, and the Henry Miller Library for something to read on the beach.

McWay Falls Nepenthe restaurant

Coastal Town · 3.5 hrs

San Luis Obispo

A Cal Poly college town that punches way above its weight. Farmers market on Thursdays shuts down Higuera St — best farmers market on the Central Coast. Downtown SLO has excellent farm-to-table restaurants and a genuinely walkable main street.

Thursday night market downtown SLO food scene

Beach Town · 15 min from SLO

Pismo Beach + Shell Beach

Classic California beach vibes — clam chowder at the pier, sand dunes you can drive on (ATVs at Oceano Dunes), cold Pacific water, and sunsets that make you dramatically reconsider your entire life.

clam chowder at the pier Oceano Dunes ATV

Wine Country · 20 min from SLO

Edna Valley + Arroyo Grande

SLO wine country is Napa without the attitude. Edna Valley Winery, Laetitia, and Talley have stunning settings and Chardonnay and Pinot that surprise people every time. Arroyo Grande's village is a bonus.

SLO wine country Pinot + Chardonnay
