Jacquie's Local Notes

  • Book Alcatraz evening tour ASAP — sells out weeks in advance
  • Angel Island ferry runs from Pier 41 — check Blue & Gold Fleet schedule
  • Muir Woods requires timed entry reservations (recreation.gov)
  • Saturday Farmer's Market at Ferry Building is 8am–2pm — get there for chilaquiles

  • 4/20 falls after they leave — Golden Gate Park Strawberry Hill is still great any day
  • Arsicault croissants: arrive before 10am or they sell out

Neighborhood Guides
Each one its own world
🏘 Inner Neighborhoods
Noe Valley
Noe Valley — The Village
SF's sunniest neighborhood (literally — it's in a microclimate pocket). 24th Street is the main drag: independent bookstores, coffee shops, wine bars, and farmers market on Saturdays. Very local, very chill.
☀️ 24th Street Farmers Market on Saturday mornings is smaller and less crowded than Ferry Building
Walk
Glen Park
Glen Park — Hidden Canyon
The most underrated neighborhood in SF. Small village feel, Glen Canyon Park (wild and woody), excellent local coffee at Glen Park Station. Feels like a town within a city.
🌿 Glen Canyon Park trail is wild and surprisingly remote feeling — 70 acres in the middle of the city
Walk
SoMa
SoMa — South of Market
SFMOMA anchor. Design district, gallery openings, excellent bars. The area around 4th and Howard has Yerba Buena Gardens (free outdoor art space), SFMOMA, and the Contemporary Jewish Museum in striking buildings.
🎨 First Thursday gallery walk in SoMa — galleries open late on first Thursdays
See
Hayes Valley
Hayes Valley — Boutique SF
Walkable neighborhood with the best independent boutiques, wine bars, and restaurants in the city. Patricia's Green (tiny park) is the neighborhood living room. Hayes Street is the main strip.
🛍 Best neighborhood for independent shopping in SF — not chains, all local
Walk
Bernal Heights
Bernal Heights — Local's Local
The neighborhood SF residents move to when they want to stay but want a real community. Cortland Avenue has independent restaurants and cafes. The hill above has the rope swing and best views. Extremely authentic.
Walk
Richmond
Inner Richmond — Fog City's Heart
Clement Street is the other Chinatown — more authentic, less touristy. Russian bakeries, Vietnamese pho spots, hole-in-the-wall dim sum, and Arsicault Bakery. The Richmond is where SF locals eat.
🥟 Clement St between 2nd and 9th Ave: best block of restaurants in SF that tourists don't visit
Eat
Outer Sunset
Outer Sunset — Surf & Fog
Where the surfers live. Irving Street and Judah Street have exceptional Asian restaurants, indie coffee, and zero tourists. Walk to Ocean Beach after. Feels like a different city.
🏄 Mollusk Surf Shop on Irving is a SF institution — great for browsing even if you don't surf
Walk
Japantown
Japantown — Peace Plaza
One of only three remaining Japantowns in the US. The Japan Center mall has incredible Japanese food stalls, bookstores, and gift shops. Benkyodo for mochi. Izakaya Rintaro for dinner.
🎎 Benkyodo on Sutter — the family has been making mochi and sembei here since 1906
See

Neighborhood Pairing Logic

  • Noe Valley + Glen Park: pair together on an afternoon, both are walkable and close
  • Hayes Valley + Japantown: 10 min walk apart, great for a full afternoon into dinner
  • Inner Richmond + Outer Sunset: natural pair on one day — Clement St lunch, Irving St dinner
  • Bernal Heights: go Sunday morning with coffee from Ritual, climb the hill, watch the rope swingers
  • SoMa: pair with the Ferry Building and Embarcadero waterfront on the same day
Museums & Culture
Beyond the obvious
🏛 Must-See Museums
GG Park
de Young Museum
World-class fine art. The observation tower is free. Check current exhibitions — they often have major touring shows. Budget 2-3 hrs.
GG Park
California Academy of Sciences
Living roof, 4-story rainforest dome, planetarium, coral reef aquarium. One of the most extraordinary science museums in the world.
SoMa
SFMOMA
Seven floors of modern and contemporary art. One of the largest MoMA museums in the world. The Richter window alone is worth the trip. Free on Thursday evenings.
Presidio
Walt Disney Family Museum
Not a theme park museum — a serious biographical institution covering Walt's life and artistic process. Stunning building inside the Presidio.
🎭 Under-the-Radar Culture
SoMa
Contemporary Jewish Museum
Daniel Libeskind-designed building that's a work of art itself. Rotating exhibitions on Jewish art, history, and culture. Often has surprisingly excellent shows.
Mission
Mission Dolores (Original Mission)
The oldest intact building in SF, built in 1776. The cemetery behind it is quiet, historic, and haunting. The painted ceiling inside is unique in California.
Pacific Heights
Haas-Lilienthal House
The only Victorian mansion open for tours in SF. Preserved from 1886 in extraordinary detail. Architecture nerds will love this.
Embarcadero
Exploratorium
Adults-only after-dark Thursdays. Interactive science museum on Pier 15 with Bay views. More fun than it sounds — genuinely mind-bending exhibits.
Castro
GLBT History Museum
First LGBTQ+ history museum in the US. Small but extraordinarily moving. Essential context for understanding the Castro and SF's history.
Presidio
Presidio Tunnel Tops
Brand new park (opened 2022) built on top of the Presidio tunnel. Stunning lawns with Golden Gate views, fire pits, a playground, and food vendors. SF's newest great public space.
Treasure Island
Treasure Island Museum
Free small museum on Treasure Island covering the 1939 World's Fair and WWII naval history. Interesting context for the island itself.
North Beach
Beat Museum
Tiny museum on Broadway dedicated to the Beat Generation — Kerouac, Ginsberg, Cassady. Artifacts, rare books, original manuscripts. Essential for literary types.
🎬 Live Culture
Castro
Castro Theatre
1922 movie palace — one of the most beautiful movie theaters in America. Check what's playing. Even if it's a random film, sit in the main house just to see the Wurlitzer organ.
Mission
Alamo Drafthouse SF
Cinema with food and drinks served at your seat. Strict no-talking policy. Best movie experience in the city.
Haight
The Fillmore
Historic music venue from the psychedelic era. If anything good is playing while CG & Bella are here, this is the venue. Free apple in the lobby is a Fillmore tradition.

🌅 Do it at golden hour. Start from the Sutro Baths parking lot and walk toward the Golden Gate.

One of the best urban hikes in America. From the Golden Gate Bridge toll plaza, descend through the old military battery tunnels, emerge on the bluffs above Baker Beach, walk the beach, and loop back. Jaw-dropping the entire way.
🏖 Pack a lunch and stop at Baker Beach to eat with the bridge above you

Twin Peaks Summit Loop
Twin summits in the geographic center of SF. The climb is exposed and windy but the views are the best in the city — on a clear day you see the Farallon Islands 27 miles offshore. Bring a layer.

4.2 mi · Moderate
Glen Canyon Park Trail
70 acres of wild canyon in the middle of the city. Feels nothing like SF — creeks, red rock formations, raptors overhead. The Billy Goat Hill staircase at the end is a local secret.
🦅 Red-tailed hawks nest here — keep an eye on the cliffs
Moderate

8.5 mi · Hard
Fort Funston → Ocean Beach → Sutro Baths
The great SF coastal walk. Start at Fort Funston's clifftop with hang gliders above you, walk 4+ miles of wild Ocean Beach, climb to the Cliff House, end at Sutro Baths ruins. Pack water and a wind layer.
🪁 Fort Funston: hang gliders launch from the bluffs — arrive early to watch them take off

Angel Island Perimeter Trail
Ferry to the island, then circumnavigate the entire thing. Every quarter mile brings a different vista — Golden Gate, Bay Bridge, Marin hills, downtown SF. The summit adds another 788ft. Best full-day outdoor adventure in the Bay.

SF Outdoors Essentials

  • Always bring a wind layer — fog and wind can appear instantly even on sunny days
  • AllTrails has offline maps for all SF trails — download before you go
  • The Marin Headlands (just over the bridge) are an extension of SF — Hawk Hill and the Tennessee Valley trail are 20 min away
  • Ocean Beach is not for swimming — rip currents are dangerous year-round
  • Golden Gate Park has 25+ miles of trails — the panhandle and east meadows are easy; the western park near the windmills is wilder
Marin County
30 min from SF · Ancient redwoods, wild coast, best views on Earth
Day Trip
🥾 Marin Hikes — The Best in the Bay Area
4.7 mi · Moderate · Marin Headlands
Hawk Hill & Marin Headlands Loop
Drive over the Golden Gate, park at Battery Spencer. Hike the headlands ridge for the most staggering Golden Gate views you'll ever see — bridge below you, city skyline behind it, Pacific in front. Connects to Rodeo Beach, a wild pebble beach at the base of the cliffs.
🦅 Sept–Nov: golden eagles and hawks migrate over Hawk Hill by the thousands. April still has raptors.
Moderate
2.4 mi · Easy · Muir Woods
Muir Woods Cathedral Grove Loop
The main loop through ancient coastal redwoods. The trees are 1,000+ years old and 250 feet tall. Cathedral Grove is genuinely awe-inspiring — silence, filtered light, immense scale. Book timed entry at recreation.gov. Takes the Dipsea Trail connection up into the hills if energy allows.
🌲 Go first thing in the morning — the light through the canopy is magical before 9am
Easy
6.2 mi · Moderate · Mount Tam
Matt Davis Trail → Bootjack → Ben Johnson Loop
The classic Mount Tamalpais loop. Traverse the south face through oak woodland and open chaparral, summit views of SF Bay, and descend through Cathedral Redwoods back to Muir Woods. Pair with a Muir Woods morning for the full Marin experience.
Moderate
8.4 mi · Strenuous · Point Reyes
Alamere Falls via Palomarin Trailhead
One of California's great hikes. The reward: a waterfall that drops directly onto a beach — a "tidefall," one of only two in California. Rollings hills, three coastal lakes, wildflowers in April, the ocean visible the whole way. Start early.
🌊 Check tide charts — low tide makes the beach at the base of the falls accessible
Strenuous
3.8 mi · Easy · Stinson Beach
Dipsea Trail to Stinson Beach
Historic trail from Mill Valley down to Stinson Beach. One of California's oldest trail races (since 1905). Drop into the famous Dipsea steps, through redwood shade, emerge at the beach. Have lunch at Stinson before hiking back or getting a shuttle.
Easy
3.5 mi · Easy · Tennessee Valley
Tennessee Valley Trail → Cove
Flat valley walk through Marin Headlands to a wild, isolated cove. No development, dramatic cliffs, crashing surf. 20 minutes from the Golden Gate. One of the most accessible true wilderness experiences near SF.
🐘 Keep eyes peeled for elephant seals on the rocks at the cove seasonally
Easy
🍽 Marin Food — Where to Eat
Mill Valley
Sol Food
Puerto Rican food that redefines the genre. Pernil, tostones, arroz con pollo, pasteles. Lines form before they open. Jacquie's top rec — mandatory.
Mill Valley
Bungalow 44
Upscale but warm neighborhood restaurant on Miller Ave. Rotisserie chicken, excellent cocktails, locally sourced seasonal menu. The best sit-down dinner in Mill Valley.
Sausalito
Fish Restaurant
All sustainable seafood on a wooden dock over the water. Clam chowder, grilled halibut, Dungeness crab. Cash only, picnic tables, incredible. Order at the counter and eat with the bay view.
Sausalito
Sushi Ran
One of the best sushi restaurants in the Bay Area. Michelin-recognized. If budget allows, this is a special meal. Reserve in advance.
Stinson Beach
Parkside Snack Bar
Classic California beach café on Stinson Beach. Fish tacos, craft burgers, local beer. Right on the sand. Post-hike perfection.
Muir Beach
The Pelican Inn
16th-century English pub replica a mile from Muir Beach. Real ale on tap, beef and kidney pie, roaring fireplace. Surreal to find in Marin but absolutely delightful.
Fairfax
Gestalt Haus
German beer hall in a tiny Marin town. 40 beers on tap, sausages, pretzels, outdoor patio. Local favorite — far from the tourist trail.
Tiburon
Sam's Anchor Café
Outdoor deck dining on the water with the SF skyline directly across the Bay. Brunch classics, fresh seafood, cold beer. One of the great Bay Area lunch spots.

Perfect Marin Day

  • 8am: Muir Woods (first entry slot) → walk Cathedral Grove
  • 11am: Drive up Mount Tam for summit views, short hike on the ridge
  • 1pm: Sol Food in Mill Valley (pernil plate, tostones, plantain, rice)

Sonoma County
1–1.5 hrs from SF · Wine, redwoods, coast, and incredible food
Day Trip
🥾 Sonoma Hikes & Outdoors
3.5 mi · Easy · Armstrong Redwoods
Armstrong Redwoods State Reserve
Near Guerneville. Coastal redwoods just as magnificent as Muir Woods but with a fraction of the visitors. No reservation required. The Colonel Armstrong Tree is 1,400 years old. Continue on the East Ridge Trail for old-growth forest and ridge views.
🌲 Free to walk in. The Icicle Tree — 308 feet tall — is at the end of the main grove trail
Easy
5.2 mi · Moderate · Bodega Head
Bodega Head Loop & Coastal Bluffs
Dramatic headland trail on the Sonoma Coast. Walk the bluff edge above crashing surf, spot gray whales (April is migration season), and look back at a wild coastline with no development in sight. One of the most raw coastal hikes in California.
🐋 April is prime gray whale migration season — bring binoculars
Moderate
4.0 mi · Easy · Doran Beach
Doran Beach & Bodega Bay
Sandy spit between the Pacific and Bodega Harbor. Walk the full length of the spit in the morning when it's quiet. Harbor seals haul out on the rocks. The town of Bodega Bay has excellent fresh seafood right off the boat.
Easy
2.0 mi · Easy · Sonoma Valley
Sonoma State Historic Park & Plaza
Walk the 8-acre town plaza — the largest historic plaza in California. The old Mexican-era mission, barracks, and vineyards are on foot. Pair with a wine tasting at one of the tasting rooms right on the square.
Easy
🍷 Wineries Worth the Visit
Healdsburg
Dry Creek Vineyard
Historic Dry Creek Valley winery. Fume blanc and zinfandel are legendary. Beautiful picnic grounds. One of Sonoma's originals, family-run since 1972.
Healdsburg
Jordan Winery
Château-style estate with olive groves and spectacular grounds. Known for Cabernet and Chardonnay. The library tasting is worth booking — older vintages in the estate library.
Sebastopol
Dutton-Goldfield Winery
Small-production Pinot Noir and Chardonnay from the cool Sonoma Coast. No appointment needed. One of the best value/quality wineries in Sonoma.
Guerneville
Korbel Champagne Cellars
Historic 1882 winery on the Russian River. Stunning estate, free tours, and their brut is genuinely good. The gardens in spring are beautiful.
🍽 Sonoma Food — Where to Eat
Healdsburg
Shed
Farm-to-table fermentation-focused café and market. The larder and pantry section is extraordinary. Breakfast and lunch only. This is what California food culture looks like at its best.
Healdsburg
Costeaux French Bakery
Healdsburg institution since 1923. Incredible croissants, morning pastries, rustic loaves. Get here for breakfast before the wineries open.
Bodega Bay
Spud Point Crab Company
Dock-side shack run by a family of local crabbers. The Dungeness crab sandwich and clam chowder are made from crabs pulled that morning. Order at the window, eat at picnic tables overlooking the marina.
Guerneville
Big Bottom Market
Tiny market-café on the Russian River. The biscuits with local honey have been featured on Food Network. Breakfast sandwiches, local provisions, incredible coffee.
Sonoma
The Girl & the Fig
Sonoma institution on the plaza. French country bistro with Rhône-focused wine list. Duck confit, charcuterie, fig & arugula salad. Book ahead for dinner.
Petaluma
Central Market
Farm-driven seasonal restaurant in a historic building in Petaluma. Hyper-local sourcing — most ingredients from within 50 miles. One of the best value fine-dining meals in the Bay Area.
Sebastopol
Ramen Gaijin
Outstanding ramen in an unlikely location — Sebastopol. Long fermented broths, local pork, house-made noodles. Often considered the best ramen outside SF proper.
Healdsburg
Oakville Grocery (Healdsburg)
Legendary California gourmet market. Build the ultimate wine-country picnic: local cheese, charcuterie, fresh bread, olives. Take it to a winery lawn.

Perfect Sonoma Day

  • 8am: Costeaux French Bakery in Healdsburg — croissant and coffee on the plaza
  • 9:30am: Armstrong Redwoods — 1,400-year-old trees, no crowds
  • Noon: Spud Point Crab Co in Bodega Bay — fresh Dungeness crab dock-side
  • 2pm: Bodega Head cliff walk — spot gray whales in April
  • 4pm: Dry Creek Vineyard tasting — zinfandel in the valley that invented it
  • 6:30pm: Dinner at Shed or The Girl & the Fig in Healdsburg/Sonoma
Santa Cruz County
1.5 hrs from SF · Surf town, redwoods, boardwalk, and killer food
Day Trip
🥾 Santa Cruz Hikes & Outdoors
5.0 mi · Moderate · Big Basin
Big Basin Redwoods State Park
California's oldest state park — old-growth redwoods some of which are 2,000 years old and 300 feet tall. The Redwood Loop Trail is 4.5 miles through the ancient grove. Note: much of the park is being restored after the 2020 CZU Lightning Fire; check current trail status at parks.ca.gov before visiting.
🌲 The Berry Creek Falls trail (11 mi round trip) rewards with three stunning waterfalls — an epic full day
Moderate
3.0 mi · Easy · West Cliff Drive
West Cliff Drive Coastal Walk
Santa Cruz's iconic coastal path runs 2.75 miles along dramatic cliffs above the Pacific. Watch surfers at Steamer Lane (one of California's most famous surf breaks), see the Surfing Museum, and end at Natural Bridges State Beach with its monarch butterfly colony in season.
🏄 Steamer Lane: some of the best surf in California. Watch from the bluff — incredible even if you don't surf.
Easy
4.5 mi · Moderate · Henry Cowell
Henry Cowell Redwoods State Park
Just north of Santa Cruz town — easier to access than Big Basin. Ancient redwood grove loop plus connector trails along the San Lorenzo River. Swim in the river pools in summer. The Pipeline Road gives a longer moderate option into the chaparral above.
Moderate
2.0 mi · Easy · Natural Bridges
Natural Bridges State Beach
Wave-cut arch over the Pacific, tide pools full of sea stars and anemones, and the world's largest overwintering monarch butterfly colony (October–February). In April it's quieter but the tide pools are outstanding. Great beach even without the butterflies.
🦋 The eucalyptus grove behind the beach has monarch butterflies year-round in smaller numbers
Easy
Beach · All Day
Capitola Village & Beginner Surf
Capitola is the most charming beach town in the Bay Area — colorful Mediterranean-style buildings, a village beach, mellow surf. Rent a board and wetsuit at O'Neill (the surf brand that started here). Capitola Beginner Surf is ideal for first-timers.
🏄 O'Neill Surf Shop is right here — this is where the wetsuit was invented
Beach
🍽 Santa Cruz Food — Where to Eat
Downtown SC
Penny Ice Creamery
Artisan small-batch ice cream made daily with local ingredients. Inventive seasonal flavors — Mexican chocolate, strawberry balsamic, cardamom rose. A Santa Cruz institution. Two locations.
Westside
Verve Coffee Roasters
Santa Cruz's cult coffee roaster, now with SF locations. The original Westside café is the best one. Single-origin pour-overs, exceptional espresso, beautiful space. A California coffee benchmark.
Downtown
Bantam
Wood-fired pizza and small plates. Local seasonal ingredients, excellent cocktails, warm atmosphere. One of the best restaurants on the Central Coast.
Eastside
Hula's Island Grill
Beloved Santa Cruz institution. Tiki cocktails, fish tacos, macadamia nut chicken. Colorful, loud, fun. Has been packed every night since 1994.
Westside
Surf City Sandwich
Local surf-culture sandwich shop. Incredible Italian sub, the Caprese, and the Veggie on housemade bread. Get it to go and eat on West Cliff while watching surfers.
Capitola
Gayle's Bakery
Legendary Capitola bakery operating since 1978. Enormous case of pastries, sandwiches, and hot food. The almond croissant and morning buns are locally iconic. Get there early.
Wharf
Stagnaro Bros.
Family-run seafood restaurant on the Santa Cruz Wharf since 1937. Cioppino, Dungeness crab, fresh fish. The old-school SF Italian seafood tradition, preserved perfectly.
Downtown
Picnic Basket
Local favourite right across from the beach. Elevated breakfast and lunch classics. The smash burger and breakfast burritos are outstanding. Casual, fast, excellent.

Perfect Santa Cruz Day

  • 8am: Verve Coffee on the Westside — best coffee in the county
  • 9am: West Cliff Drive walk — watch surfers at Steamer Lane from the bluff
  • 11am: Henry Cowell Redwoods — old-growth grove, river swimming
  • 1pm: Gayle's Bakery in Capitola for a picnic lunch, eat on the village beach
  • 3pm: Rent a board/wetsuit, try beginner surf at Capitola
  • 5pm: Natural Bridges tide pools before the light goes
  • 7pm: Bantam for wood-fired dinner and cocktails downtown
Peninsula & Santa Clara
30–60 min from SF · Redwood trails, farm stands, coastal gems
Day Trip
🥾 Peninsula Hikes — Underrated & Outstanding
6.5 mi · Moderate · Portola Redwoods
Portola Redwoods State Park
La Honda, 45 min from SF. Deep canyon redwoods with almost no visitors — one of the least-known old-growth groves in California. The Sequoia Nature Trail connects to the Summit Trail for ridge views. Completely different feel from Muir Woods — wild and quiet.
🌲 Backcountry camping available — this is one of the most beautiful overnight spots in the Bay Area
Moderate
4.2 mi · Moderate · Purisima Creek
Purisima Creek Redwoods Open Space
Halfmoon Bay hills above the coast. The canyon drops 1,500 feet through ancient redwoods to the coast below. On clear days you can see the Farallon Islands from the ridge. One of the most beautiful open-space preserves in the Bay Area and almost nobody knows about it.
Moderate
3.0 mi · Easy · Año Nuevo
Año Nuevo State Park — Elephant Seals
1.5 hrs south of SF. Guided tours let you walk within feet of thousands of elephant seals on the beach. The males can be 5,000 lbs. One of the most extraordinary wildlife encounters in California. April: post-breeding season, juveniles still present. Book tours at reserveamerica.com.
🦭 Not April peak season but juveniles ("weaners") are still on the beach — still spectacular
Wildlife
5.0 mi · Moderate · Monte Bello
Monte Bello Open Space (Above Palo Alto)
Ridge above the Santa Clara Valley. Walk the San Andreas Fault directly — you can see the offset in the landscape. Views of the entire Bay, South Bay, and coastal hills. The Stevens Creek headwaters trail connects through wildflower meadows in April.
🌸 April: this ridge has spectacular wildflower blooms — poppies, lupine, owl's clover
Moderate
2.0 mi · Easy · Pescadero
Pescadero Marsh & Bean Hollow Beach
Small coastal marsh full of shore birds just above a driftwood-strewn beach. Take Pescadero Road from Hwy 1. Walk the marsh boardwalk, then Bean Hollow State Beach (dramatic sculptured sandstone) five minutes south. Pair with a stop in Pescadero town.
Easy
🍽 Peninsula & Santa Clara Food
Pescadero
Duarte's Tavern
Family-run since 1894. The artichoke soup is made from artichokes grown in the field next door. Green chile omelettes, Olallieberry pie, local crab. A National Register-listed landmark that still serves outstanding food.
Half Moon Bay
Barbara's Fish Trap
Tiny roadside shack on the water at Princeton Harbor. The fresh fish and chips and cioppino are made from catches landed that morning at the dock 50 yards away.
Half Moon Bay
Pasta Moon
Italian trattoria that's been a Half Moon Bay anchor since 1988. Handmade pasta, local Dungeness crab, wood oven. The butternut squash ravioli with sage brown butter is legendary locally.
San Jose
Original Joe's
Italian-American institution in downtown San Jose since 1956. The meatballs, veal parmigiana, and the whole supper-club atmosphere feel like a time capsule. Go for the experience.
Palo Alto
Tamarine
Contemporary Vietnamese in Palo Alto. Elegant, creative, and far better than the surrounding Silicon Valley food scene deserves. The shaking beef and caramelized clay pot fish are standouts.
San Jose
Santana Row
Open-air dining street in San Jose with a surprisingly good mix of restaurants. Ozumo for sushi, La Strada for Italian, and ROW Bar for cocktails — all in a walkable European-style block.
San Jose
Pho Kim Long
San Jose's Vietnamese community (the largest outside Vietnam) means extraordinary pho. On Story Road in East San Jose — the real deal, massive portions, open at 7am.
Los Altos
Cascal
Pan-Latin tapas in a lively setting. The ceviche, empanadas, and sangria flight are all excellent. Peninsula locals have been coming here for 20 years.
🌾 Peninsula Farm Stands & Markets
Half Moon Bay
Half Moon Bay Farmers Market
Saturday mornings on Main Street. The coast's microclimate grows exceptional Brussels sprouts, artichokes, and strawberries. In April: first strawberries of the season.
San Martin
Gizdich Ranch
U-pick apple and berry farm in the Santa Cruz Mountains. The homemade pies are locally famous. Pure California farm experience, 50 min south of SF.
Pescadero
Harley Farms Goat Dairy
Artisan goat cheese farm on the coast. Tours on weekends. The fresh chèvre and aged rounds are sold at the farm store. One of the most beautiful farms in California.

Perfect Peninsula Day

  • 9am: Drive Hwy 1 south from SF — cliff edge, artichoke farms, pumpkin fields
  • 10am: Purisima Creek Redwoods hike — 4 miles through ancient canyon redwoods
  • 1pm: Duarte's Tavern in Pescadero — artichoke soup, fish, olallieberry pie
  • 3pm: Bean Hollow Beach + Pescadero Marsh birdwatching
  • 5pm: Half Moon Bay Main Beach sunset, stroll the town
  • 7pm: Pasta Moon for dinner — handmade pasta and local crab
