Welcome to The Bay

This is a living menu, not a schedule. Use it to build your own days based on how you feel. Mix and match. Wake up slow and do one thing beautifully, or go hard and eat your way across three counties. The Bay rewards both.

🇵🇭 A Note for the Filipinos in the Room

The Bay Area is home to the second-largest Filipino population in the United States. SoMa Pilipinas is an official cultural district. Daly City — a 15-minute BART ride away — is called "the most Filipino city outside the Philippines." From turo-turo counters to Michelin-adjacent fine dining, the diaspora is fully present here. You are home. Eat accordingly.

Ways to Do It
All suggestions are optional. Sleep in. Pivot. Eat the same thing twice if it's that good. That's the local way.
How to Navigate
For Foodies
Follow the Food Tabs
Start with Eat & Drink for the classics, then go deep in Asian Diaspora, Latin & More, and Hole in the Wall. The Bay's food scene runs on immigrant cooking — that thread leads everywhere good.
For Nature

Hike & Wildlife has sea otters, elephant seals, and gray whale migration (April peak). Coastal Drive maps Highway 1 from Pacifica all the way to Pismo Beach — the world's best coastal road.
For Culture
Events & Neighborhoods

Road Trip
Drive the Coast
The farthest you'd go: San Luis Obispo & Pismo Beach — 3.5 hrs down Hwy 1 through Big Sur. The most unforgettable drive in California, doable as an overnight.

Day Ideas

These are vibes, not itineraries. Take one, split one across two days, or combine two into a wild marathon. The Bay rewards flexibility above everything.

City Days — San Francisco
☀️Morning to Afternoon
The Ferry Building Morning
Embarcadero → North Beach
Saturday/Tuesday farmers market at the Ferry Building — Cowgirl Creamery, Blue Bottle, Hog Island oysters at the bar counter, the chilaquiles vendor with the early-morning line. Walk the Embarcadero north to Pier 39 to see the sea lions (loudest before 10am). Coffee at Café Trieste in North Beach — the owner might break into opera. City Lights bookstore. Lunch at Molinari Deli on Columbus.
🦭 Sea lions at Pier 39 K-dock — up to 900 hauled out, peak season right now
Half or Full Day
Golden Gate & the Presidio
Crissy Field → Bridge → Baker Beach
Croissant from Arsicault (open 7am), then walk Crissy Field's restored tidal marsh and the Wave Organ (acoustic sea sculpture made from pipes and marble). Walk across the Golden Gate Bridge — 1.7 miles, 40 minutes, the greatest urban walk anywhere. Continue to Baker Beach under the south tower. End at the Walt Disney Family Museum in the Presidio — the most underrated museum in SF.
🌉 Check fog forecast — the bridge disappearing into fog is just as magnificent
Morning → Sunset
Chinatown, Cable Cars & Painted Ladies
Nob Hill → Western Addition
Start at Good Mong Kok Bakery for egg tarts ($1 each). Walk Ross Alley for the fortune cookie factory — watch them folded by hand on iron presses. Cable car from Powell to Fisherman's Wharf (not fast, entirely worth it). Afternoon in Haight-Ashbury — Amoeba Records is the best record store in the US. Golden hour at Alamo Square: Painted Ladies with the skyline behind them.
Outdoor Day
Land's End + The Outer Sunset
Sea Cliffs → Ocean Beach
Land's End trail (2.2mi) along sea cliffs from Sutro Baths ruins — there are ruins of a Greek temple in the cypress forest, hidden beach coves below. Walk Ocean Beach south to the Dutch Windmills at the park entrance. Lunch at Outerlands or Devil's Teeth Baking Co. Bike or walk through Golden Gate Park. The Outer Sunset at dusk feels like a secret the rest of SF doesn't know about.
🌊 Sutro Baths: free to walk, best at low tide when the tide pools are exposed
Culture Day
Golden Gate Park Museums
de Young → Cal Academy → Tea Garden
The de Young museum (rooftop tower: free 360° city views). The California Academy of Sciences next door — living rainforest, African penguin exhibit, and the best natural history museum west of Chicago. Japanese Tea Garden is 5 minutes away. Strawberry Hill island in Stow Lake. Dutch Windmills at sunset close by. This is the SF most visitors skip — it's the best part.
🎨 SFMOMA free every Thursday 5–9pm — pair with SoMa dinner
Neighborhood Wander
The Mission All Day
Murals → Tacos → Dolores Park
Start with chilaquiles at El Combre (arrive early). Walk Balmy Alley and Clarion Alley — the best outdoor murals in the US, politics and beauty on every wall. La Palma Mexicatessen for hot tortillas off the press. Bi-Rite Creamery for ice cream. Afternoon at Dolores Park with the whole city spread out below you. Tartine bread at 5pm. Dinner at any taqueria on Mission St.
🌮 La Taqueria (James Beard award) — the OG Mission burrito: no rice, no filler
Special Night
Alcatraz Evening Tour
Book Weeks Ahead · Pier 33
The night tour is the one — haunting audio tour through the darkened cellhouse. The boat ride itself gives the most dramatic SF skyline views of your trip. Departs Pier 33 after 5:45pm, returns ~8:30pm. Pre-dinner at Swan Oyster Depot (lunch only — plan an early day). Post-Alcatraz: Smuggler's Cove tiki bar for a fire bowl cocktail.
🚤 alcatrazcruises.com — sells out 3–4 weeks ahead without fail
Neighborhood Morning
Pacific Heights + Japantown
Fillmore → Pac Heights → Japantown
The best brunch corridor in SF is Fillmore Street in Pacific Heights — Sweet Maple or The Grove for breakfast. Walk the Victorian mansion streets toward the bay for the architecture alone. Into Japantown: Benkyodo mochi shop (since 1906, hand-rolled with 120-year-old recipes), Kinokuniya bookshop, Japan Center. Then the Lyon Street Steps (220 stone stairs, panoramic bay view at the top) — the best free view in SF.
Oakland
First Friday · April 3
Oakland First Friday — Oakland Blooms 🌸
Telegraph Ave · Free · 5–9:30pm
The Bay Area's best free street festival. Five blocks of Telegraph Ave transform — galleries, live music, DJs, spoken word, local food vendors, street artists, up to 30,000 people. BART to 19th St Oakland. This is Oakland at full power: raw, creative, electric, genuinely free. Combine with a Fruitvale taco run beforehand or a post-festival late dinner at Miss Ollie's.
🎨 Oakland Art Murmur gallery walk runs the same night — galleries open late
Any Day
Fruitvale Village + Lake Merritt
BART to Fruitvale
Fruitvale Village is Oakland's most authentic Mexican food corridor — the best birria, carnitas, and Oaxacan mole in the Bay Area at $3 taco prices. Saturday morning street market. After: drive or BART to Lake Merritt — a tidal lagoon in the center of Oakland, with a 3-mile walking path, farmer's market Saturday mornings, and kayak rentals on the water.
Flexible Add-Ons
Bay Experience
Angel Island Ferry Day
The largest natural island in SF Bay. 5-mile perimeter trail, 360° views of SF, Marin, and the East Bay. Immigration Station (the "Ellis Island of the West") — Chinese exclusion era history told with extraordinary care. Bike rentals on island. Ferry from Pier 41 or Tiburon.
Easter Sunday 🐣
Dolores Park Easter
The Mission turns Dolores Park into an only-in-SF outdoor party on Easter — the Hunky Jesus pageant happens here. The whole neighborhood comes out. Brunch at any Mission spot. The park has the best skyline view in SF and is at its most alive all year on this day.
Afternoon
Treasure Island at Sunset
The man-made island in the middle of the Bay Bridge. Pull off on your way back from Oakland for the jaw-dropping SF skyline view that photographers fight over. Entirely free. Small Sunday farmers market. The city at sunset from the water is the best possible last image of a day.

SF Neighborhoods

Every neighborhood in SF has its own accent. These are the ones worth dedicating real time to — and what to do once you're there.

Outer Sunset
The Outer Sunset
Surfers, fog, ramen shops, boba, Devil's Teeth Baking, Ocean Beach. The most genuinely local part of SF — nobody comes here to see anything. That's the point.
Mission District
La Misión
Murals, taquerias, Tartine, Dolores Park. Sunniest microclimate in SF. The Mission burrito was born here.
Near Fisherman's Wharf
North Beach
Beat poets, Italian espresso, City Lights bookstore, Vesuvio bar. Coit Tower above. The most literary neighborhood in America.
Inner Richmond
Clement Street
SF's second Chinatown, plus Vietnamese, Russian, Korean. Best restaurant street for value and variety in the city. Green Apple Books here is a SF institution.
Pacific Heights
Pac Heights + Japantown
Victorian mansions, Fillmore brunch corridor, Japantown. Lyon Street steps: best free view in SF. Architecturally stunning neighborhood to just walk.
Castro District
The Castro
LGBTQ+ cultural capital of the world. The Castro Theatre is a working movie palace. Harvey Milk's legacy is present on every corner.
SoMa / SOMA Pilipinas
SoMa
Filipino cultural district, SFMOMA, galleries, live music venues, 4505 BBQ. The gritty creative heart of new SF.
Noe Valley
Noe Valley
The sunny village inside the city. Saturday farmers market on 24th St. Excellent coffee, bookshops, and the least tourist-facing vibe in SF.
Hayes Valley
Hayes Valley
Most boutique-forward neighborhood. Patricia's Green park, Sightglass Coffee, Absinthe Bar, natural wine, independent fashion.
Western Addition
Japantown
One of three remaining Japantowns in the US. Benkyodo mochi (since 1906), Japan Center, Kinokuniya bookstore, ramen everywhere.

What's On

Events happening during and around your late-March to early-April visit. April is one of the best months in the Bay.

🔥 Don't Miss
🎨
April 3 · Oakland · Free · 5–9:30pm
Oakland First Friday — "Oakland Blooms" 🌸
Telegraph Ave from 27th to West Grand becomes the Bay Area's best free street festival. Art galleries open late, live music, DJs, food vendors, spoken word, street artists, up to 30,000 people. BART to 19th St Oakland. The April theme is Oakland Blooms. Runs every first Friday monthly.
🐣
Easter Sunday · Dolores Park · Free · Noon onward
Easter at Dolores Park
The Mission turns Dolores Park into an only-in-SF outdoor celebration. The legendary Hunky Jesus pageant. The whole neighborhood out, BBQs, music, the park packed with color. Brunch beforehand at any Mission taqueria. The park is at its most alive and joyful of the year on this day.
⚾
April 2026 · Oracle Park · Embarcadero
SF Giants Home Games
Oracle Park is consistently voted the most beautiful baseball stadium in America — right on McCovey Cove with the bay behind right field. Even as a non-baseball person: garlic fries, local craft beer, that view. Check mlb.com/giants for the April home schedule.
🏀
Late March–April · Chase Center · Mission Bay
Golden State Warriors
Chase Center in Mission Bay — walkable from downtown. The Warriors' home atmosphere is among the best in the NBA. Check nba.com/warriors for games. Upper deck tickets are affordable; the experience is excellent anywhere in the arena.
Every Week
🌾
Saturday 8am–2pm · Ferry Building · Year-round
Ferry Building Farmers Market
California's best farmers market. Cowgirl Creamery, Hog Island raw bar, first April strawberries, Frog Hollow stone fruit, the chilaquiles vendor with the pre-9am line. Also runs Tuesdays 10am–2pm (smaller, calmer, local crowd). The whole Embarcadero waterfront comes alive.
🎵
Ongoing · Various Venues
Live Music — The Fillmore, The Independent, The Fox Oakland
The Fillmore (Fillmore & Geary) is the legendary SF venue — every major artist in history has played here, the walls covered in original concert posters. The Independent on Divisadero, Great American Music Hall, The Fox Theater in Oakland. Check sfgate.com/music or Bandsintown for April shows.
🌙
Every Friday April onwards · OMCA · Oakland · 5–9pm
Friday Nights at OMCA with Off the Grid
Oakland Museum of California's beloved seasonal block party resumes in April. Free live music on the lawn, Off the Grid food trucks, galleries open late. The museum has extraordinary California history and contemporary art. museumca.org for details.
🛒
Saturday Mornings · Various
Neighborhood Farmers Markets
Noe Valley 24th St (8am–1pm) — the most neighborhood-local market in SF. Castro Farmers Market (Noe & Market). Clement Street (Inner Richmond, Sundays). Oakland Grand Lake Market (Saturday, very good). Marin Civic Center (San Rafael, Sat–Sun) — one of California's best.
Nature Events — April Specifically
🐋
March–April · Sonoma Coast · Point Reyes
Gray Whale Migration — Peak Season
Up to 26,000 gray whales pass the California coast northbound in April heading from Baja Mexico to Alaska. Best land viewing: Bodega Head (Sonoma), Chimney Rock at Point Reyes, Marin Headlands. Bring binoculars. Sanctuary Cruises from Moss Landing offers guided tours.
🌸
April · Marin Headlands · Point Reyes · Sonoma Coast
California Wildflower Season — Peak
April is peak wildflower season — lupine, California poppies, and owl's clover carpet the Marin Headlands and Sonoma Coast. Drive any coastal road in early April morning light for the full effect. The hillsides above the Pacific turn purple and orange.

Eat & Drink

SF is one of the world's great food cities — built on California produce, immigrant cooking, and a culture that has always taken restaurants seriously. This is the overview. The real depth is in the Asian Diaspora, Latin, and Hole in the Wall tabs.

Asian Diaspora

The Bay Area is the greatest concentration of Asian-American food culture in the Western world — Vietnamese refugees who arrived after 1975, the Filipino diaspora across SoMa and Daly City, multiple Chinese communities, Japanese Americans in Japantown, Koreans in Oakland, South Indian engineers in Sunnyvale. This is the food Bourdain came here specifically to eat.

🇵🇭 Filipino First

SF has the second-largest Filipino population in the US. SoMa Pilipinas is an official cultural district. Daly City (15 min by BART) is called "the most Filipino city outside the Philippines." The food spans turo-turo counters, Filipino-Mexican fusion, fine dining tasting menus, and everything between. From adobo to lechon kawali to halo-halo — you are home.

🇵🇭 Filipino
Fisherman's Wharf · Fine Dining · Book Ahead
ABACÁ ⭐
Contemporary Filipino-Californian · Kimpton Alton
The finest Filipino restaurant in California. Chef Francis & Dian Ang: sisig fried rice, ube cheesecake, buko pie, lumpia with California produce. The attached Panaderia Abacá does ensaymada, bibingka, and Filipino pastry all day. Book ahead — fills every night. restaurantabaca.com
Mission · Valencia St · Fusion
Señor Sisig
Filipino-Mexican Fusion · Bay Area Original
Sisig on a burrito, sisig in a bowl, sisig on everything. The Filipino-Mexican mashup that became a Bay Area institution. Pork and chicken sisig cooked citrusy and crispy, stuffed into a Mission-style burrito.
Tenderloin · 237 Ellis St
Kusina Ni Tess
Authentic Home-Style · Breakfast All Day
Tapsilog, adobo, laing, dinuguan, sinigang — all cooked like nanay made it. Breakfast served all day. Under $15. The best argument that SF's greatest meals aren't in fancy restaurants.
Daly City · BART to Colma · Cash Only
Chibog (Daly City) ⭐
Turo-Turo · Point-Point Cafeteria
The turo-turo experience — point at what you want behind the glass. Sizzling sisig with fried egg, char-grilled skewers, kare-kare. Cash only, no seating. Daly City is the capital of Filipino America. Make the trip.
Mission · Filipino-American Brunch
Alnico (1050 Valencia)
Filipino-American Brunch · Photo-Worthy
Ube hotcakes, sweet-and-savory combos that show how Filipino culture evolves without losing its soul. The place that makes Filipino-American food cool and deeply good simultaneously.
Special Occasion · Tasting Menu
Restaurant Naides
Filipino Fine Dining
SF's rare Filipino tasting menu experience. Traces the Philippine archipelago through modern technique — treated with the same precision as French or Japanese cooking. The celebration dinner.

The pho that changed SF — Northern-style (clear, fragrant, intensely savory), not the southern bowls most Westerners know. Steven Pham's 1999 institution returned in 2025 after a 2-year gap. Bún thang, pandan waffle to finish.

Chef Helene An's garlic noodles (invented ~1978) made in a literal kitchen-within-a-kitchen that only the An family enters. The roasted Dungeness crab with those noodles is the order. Moved to a stunning new FiDi location in late 2025.

Pâté, pork, pickled daikon, jalapeño, cilantro on a perfect baguette. Under $6. One of the great sandwiches in the world at any price. Always a lunch line. Always worth it.

Thick egg noodles, braised duck leg or char siu. Order dry (khô) with soup on the side. The cuisine of ethnic Chinese Vietnamese communities — one of the most interesting, underknown noodle shops in SF.

Huge portions, very reasonable prices. The bún bò Huế (spicy Central Vietnamese beef noodle soup) is one of the best in the city. Pairs perfectly with a walk along Clement Street's incredible restaurant corridor.

Modern Vietnamese · Inner Richmond
Garlic noodles, Hanoi BBQ platter, whole fried fish. Clement St between 1st–9th Ave is the best street in SF for grazing — Vietnamese, Chinese, Korean, Russian all on one corridor.

🍛 Indian South Bay — The Sunnyvale Pilgrimage

Why the South Bay?

Sunnyvale, Santa Clara, Fremont, and San Jose have the highest concentration of South Asian immigrants in the US outside New Jersey — and consequently some of the finest Indian regional cooking available outside India. Dosas taller than your arm. Hyderabadi biryani cooked properly. Mumbai street chaat. 45 min by Caltrain from SF, or a 35-min drive south on 101. Completely worth it.

Sunnyvale · Vegetarian South Indian
Chaat Bhavan / Dosa Place ⭐
Masala Dosa · Mumbai Street Chaat
The masala dosa (fermented rice-lentil crepe with potato filling) served with three chutneys and sambhar: one of the great breakfasts of the world. Chaat: pani puri, bhel puri, dahi papdi — explosive. Under $12. Always packed with Indian families — the highest possible endorsement.
Mountain View · Upscale
Sakoon (Mountain View)
Modern Indian · Weekend Buffet
The most acclaimed upscale Indian in the South Bay. Butter chicken and saag paneer with genuine refinement, alongside egg curry and bread pakora. The weekend buffet is constantly fresh and rotating.
San Jose · Santana Row
Amber India (Santana Row)
Modern Indian · Cocktail Program
Elegant curries, excellent tandoori, strong cocktail program in San Jose's chic Santana Row. Best option for a proper Indian dinner without driving further into the suburbs.

Latin & More

The Mission District is the Latin cultural heart of SF — and has been since long before tech arrived. The Bay's Latin food scene extends to Oakland's Fruitvale, the most authentic Mexican food corridor on the West Coast.

🇲🇽 Mexican & Latin SF
Mission · 2889 Mission · James Beard Award
La Taqueria ⭐
The OG Mission Burrito · No Rice · No Filler
The James Beard America's Classic winner. The carne asada or carnitas burrito: meat, beans, cheese, salsa — no rice, no filler. A philosophical position on burritos. Endless daily lines. This is the ur-burrito.
Mission · Since 1953
La Palma Mexicatessen
Tortilla Factory · Hot Off the Press
Masa and tortillas made fresh all day — buy them hot off the press. Pupusas, tamales, handmade tortillas with carnitas. The salsas are some of the finest in the city. Living Mission heritage since 1953.
Mission · Weekend Brunch
El Combre
Mexican Brunch · Local Neighborhood Secret
The Mission's local favorite for weekend Mexican brunch. Huevos rancheros, chilaquiles, tamales from recipes that go back generations. No tourist markup, no pretension. Busy on weekends — arrive early.
Mission · Vegan Mexican
Gracias Madre
Plant-Based Mexican · Beautiful Patio
Plant-based Mexican so good you forget it's vegan. Jackfruit carnitas, mushroom ceviche, coconut mole. Beautiful Mission patio. The mezcal cocktail list — particularly the spiced tamarind margarita — is outstanding.
Mission · Late Night
Taqueria Cancún
Super Burrito · Open Late
La Taqueria's rival — this one has rice, the super burrito style. The carnitas, al pastor, and carne asada are all excellent. Open late. The Mission at midnight: this is where you go.
Mission · Salvadoran
El Zocalo
Pupusas · Most Underrated Mission Cuisine
The Mission's best Salvadoran food. Pupusas (stuffed corn cakes with cheese, loroco, or pork) served with curtido and salsa. Under $4 each. The most underrated cuisine in the Mission.
Oakland · Fruitvale — The Real Thing
Fruitvale Village, Oakland — BART to Fruitvale Station. The most authentic Mexican food corridor in the Bay Area. Taquerias, panaderías, carnicerias along International Boulevard. Oaxacan mole, Jalisco birria, Sinaloa-style mariscos. $3 tacos. Saturday mornings the street market is at full power. This is where Bay Area Mexicans go when they want the real thing. Bourdain stopped on International Blvd during his Parts Unknown Bay Area episode and ate tacos from an unnamed cart. That unnamed cart energy is everywhere here.

Peruvian rotisserie chicken with aji amarillo sauce, fresh ceviche, causa. One of the great cheap meals in SF — the chicken and green sauce alone is worth the trip.

901 Washington · Caribbean Soul Food

SF-Style Burritos · Mission & SoMa
Consistently ranked among SF's best burritos. The tomato salsa is the secret — chunky, fresh, housemade daily. Healthier-leaning versions of Mission classics. Local cult following for years.

The Bourdain Thread

Bourdain filmed four separate episodes in San Francisco across all his shows. Every time he followed the same instinct: skip the trendy spots, find the counter with 18 seats and a 100-year-old menu. The places below are, in part, a map of where he led — though we're not making a big thing of it.

The Counter Institutions
1517 Polk St · Cash Only · Mon–Sat 8am–2:30pm
Swan Oyster Depot ⭐
Since 1912 · 18 Marble Seats · No Reservations
Same family since 1912. Hand-written chalkboard menu. Oysters pulled that morning. Dungeness crab back. Clam chowder. Sicilian crudo. Arrive by 9am or expect a serious wait. CASH ONLY. Closed Sundays. One of the most important counters in American food. Andrew Zimmern called the crab back "unicorn juice."
Van Ness · Silver Cart Service Since 1949
House of Prime Rib ⭐
1940s SF Glamour · Book Weeks Ahead
Whole roasts carved tableside from silver domed carts. Yorkshire pudding. Spun tableside salad. 1940s SF glamour that never ended. Bourdain lost his mind here over a martini and a half-pound slab of beef. Locals book this for every special occasion.
FiDi · Since 1849 · Oldest in California
Tadich Grill ⭐
Operating Since the Gold Rush
California's oldest restaurant. Hangtown Fry (oysters, bacon, eggs — invented here during the Gold Rush). Dark wood paneling, counter seating, waiters who've been there 30 years. Nothing has changed. Everything is the point.
Pier 30 · Embarcadero · Since 1955
Red's Java House
Waterfront Shack · Bay Bridge Views
A shack where the dockworkers ate. Cheeseburger on sourdough, cheap beer, the Bay Bridge from the railing. Bourdain called it the "American Dream right here." Come for breakfast on a weekday morning before anyone else is there.
Embarcadero · Outdoor Bar
Pier 23 Café
Bay Views · Local Crowd · Live Music Weekends
Bourdain's recommended alternative to Fisherman's Wharf: "If the weather is nice, Pier 23 might be what you need." Waterfront bar right on the Embarcadero. Cold beer, live music, bay view without the tourist price.
Divisadero · Presidential Platter
4505 Burgers & BBQ
Texas-Style BBQ · SF's Best
The Presidential Platter: brisket, smoked chicken, pork ribs, jalapeño cheddar hot links, chicharrones, coleslaw, pozole, fries. Bourdain: "Life is suddenly very, very good." SF's best BBQ. Communal tables, smoke drifting across Divisadero.

The oldest Japanese-American confectionery in the US — survived the 1906 earthquake, survived WWII internment, survived a century of SF change. Hand-rolled mochi and manju. Bubblegum, strawberry, red bean. Buy a box and eat it on the street.

Hike & Wildlife

April is the best month to be outside in the Bay Area. Wildflowers on the Marin Headlands. Sea otter pups at Elkhorn Slough. Gray whale migration at peak. Elephant seal pups weaning at Año Nuevo. You're here at exactly the right time.

🦭 Wildlife — April Is Prime Season
Year-round · Free
Sea Lions at Pier 39
Year's Peak: Nov–May Right Now
California sea lions colonized Pier 39's K-dock after the 1989 earthquake and never left. Up to 900 haul out, bark, and sleep in the sun. Marine Mammal Center volunteers explain everything. Free. Best at dawn before tourists arrive.
🦦Moss Landing · 1.5 hrs
Sea Otters at Elkhorn Slough
Kayak Tour · elkhornsloughsafari.com
The highest concentration of sea otters in California. In spring, mothers float on their backs with pups on their chests in the kelp. Kayak tours put you eye-level with the otters, sea lions, and harbor seals. Mothers crack shellfish on their chests while nursing. An extraordinary experience.
🚣 Book morning pontoon tour — elkhornsloughsafari.com
Point Reyes · 1.5 hrs
Elephant Seals at Chimney Rock
Cliff Viewpoint · Free · Point Reyes NS
Hundreds of elephant seals hauled out on a beach below — viewed from a cliff trail. In April the young of the year are weaning. Point Reyes also has tule elk on Tomales Point, harbor seals at Drakes Beach, and gray whales from the lighthouse trail. One of the most biodiverse coastal parks in the US.
Año Nuevo · 1.5 hrs
Elephant Seals at Año Nuevo
Self-Guided April · reserveamerica.com
In April self-guided walking begins — walk among hundreds of Northern elephant seals on the beach. Males can weigh 2,000 lbs and look genuinely prehistoric up close. January-born pups are weaning and learning to swim. One of the most extraordinary close-contact wildlife experiences in North America.
Sonoma Coast · 1.5 hrs
Gray Whale Migration
Bodega Head · Point Reyes · April Peak
April is peak northbound migration — up to 26,000 gray whales heading from Baja Mexico to Alaska. Best land viewing: Bodega Head cliff walk (Sonoma), Chimney Rock (Point Reyes), Marin Headlands. Look for the grey spout and rounded dark back rolling through the swell. Binoculars essential.
Monterey · 2 hrs
Marine Life at Monterey Bay
Kayak · Aquarium · Cannery Row
Kayak from Cannery Row: paddle through kelp forests with sea otters floating alongside, harbor seals, sea lions. The Monterey Bay Aquarium sits literally above the bay — waves come through the floor vents. Hopkins Marine Life Refuge has harbor seals nursing pups in April yards from downtown.
🦦 Otters float 50 yards from the kayak launch — montereybayaquarium.org
🥾 Best Hikes — SF, Marin & Point Reyes
SF · 2.2 miles · Easy
Land's End Trail
Sutro Baths → Eagles Point Overlook
The best urban hike in SF. Sea cliffs, hidden beach coves, ruins of Sutro Baths, ruins of a Greek temple in the cypress forest. Golden Gate views across the channel at every turn. April wildflowers on the headlands. Free. Dramatic. Don't skip it.
Marin · 4.7 miles · Moderate
Hawk Hill + Rodeo Beach
Golden Gate NRA · Battery Spencer · Seals
Drive over the Golden Gate, park at Battery Spencer. Ridge walk with the bridge directly below you. Hawk Hill: April wildflowers, golden eagles on migration. Descend to Rodeo Beach — colored serpentine pebbles, harbor seals on rocks at Rodeo Lagoon. The Marin Headlands hike.
Marin · 3.5 miles · Easy
Tennessee Valley
Marin Headlands → Isolated Cove
Flat valley walk through Marin Headlands grasslands to a completely isolated Pacific cove. April: lupine and poppies, red-tailed hawks, harbor seals occasionally from the clifftop. 20 minutes from the Golden Gate, no crowds on weekday mornings.
Marin · 6.2 miles · Moderate-Hard
Mount Tam — Matt Davis Trail
Stinson Beach → Ben Johnson Loop
The classic Mount Tamalpais traverse through redwood forest and chaparral ridges. Bay views that extend across the entire region on clear days. Connects to Muir Woods at the bottom. April is peak: flowers everywhere, streams full. Swim at Stinson Beach after if warm enough.
Marin · 2.4 miles · Easy
Muir Woods Cathedral Grove
Old-Growth Redwoods · Book Entry First
Trees up to 1,000 years old, 250 feet tall. A cathedral of silence. The Cathedral Grove loop takes 90 minutes and is completely unlike any other experience near SF. Book timed entry at recreation.gov — required. Weekday mornings: you may have the grove to yourself.
Point Reyes · 8.4 miles · Moderate
Alamere Falls via Palomarin
Tidefall Waterfall Directly onto Beach
One of only two tidefall waterfalls in the US — water dropping directly onto a Pacific beach. Hike through coastal scrub, past lakes and lagoons, reaching the falls at the end. April: wildflowers at peak, streams full. A full day out from SF. Worth every step.

The Coastal Drive

Highway 1 south from San Francisco to Pismo Beach is one of the world's great road trips. Drive it in pieces or do the whole thing. Every pullout has a view worth stopping for — drive south in the morning for the best light.

🚗 The Route: SF → Pacifica → Half Moon Bay → Santa Cruz → Monterey → Big Sur → SLO/Pismo

Total drive time SF to Pismo Beach: ~4 hours without stops. You will stop constantly. Ideal as two separate day trips or a one-night coastal trip. The Big Sur stretch between Carmel and San Simeon is the most dramatic section — drive it in daylight.

Pacifica to Half Moon Bay — 20–45 min from SF
Pacifica State Beach
20 min · Surfing · Sea Cliffs
The first major beach south of SF — wide, wild, often uncrowded. Surfers year-round. The sea cliffs above the beach have walking trails with dramatic views. Pacifica taco trucks on Palmetto Ave are the secret food find on this stretch.
Rockaway Beach + Pedro Point Headlands
25 min · Hidden Coves · Wildflowers
Pull off at Pedro Point Headlands between Pacifica and Half Moon Bay for coastal trail with wildflowers, tide pools, and almost no one. The hidden beach coves below are accessible with a scramble. April wildflowers here are exceptional.
Sam's Chowder House ⭐
Half Moon Bay · 4210 N Cabrillo Hwy · Lunch Destination
Perched on an oceanside bluff right above the Pacific. The lobster roll is one of the five best sandwiches in America (Today Show, SF Magazine). Award-winning clam chowder. Fire pits, Adirondack chairs, ocean view. Come for lunch, stay two hours. Dogs welcome on patio. Voted Best Seafood and Best Outdoor Dining in the Bay Area. Book ahead for weekends.
Half Moon Bay State Beaches + Mavericks
45 min · 4 Miles of Sand · April Whale Watching
Four connected state beaches — Francis, Venice, Dunes, Pelican Point. April gray whale migration makes this excellent for land-based whale watching. Saturday farmers market in town (first artichokes, strawberries). Mavericks big-wave surf break is nearby — the famous spot, viewable from shore.
Pescadero to Santa Cruz
Pescadero & Duarte's Tavern
1 hr · Artichoke Country · Since 1894
A farming village that barely knows tourists exist. Duarte's Tavern has been serving artichoke soup and olallieberry pie since 1894. Pescadero Marsh bird sanctuary. Pigeon Point Lighthouse on Hwy 1 — one of the tallest on the West Coast.
Año Nuevo State Park
1.5 hrs · Elephant Seals · Self-Guided in April
Walk among hundreds of elephant seals on the beach — males up to 2,000 lbs, pups learning to swim in the shallows. One of the most extraordinary wildlife encounters available without a plane. Combine with Duarte's lunch on the same drive. reserveamerica.com
Santa Cruz
1.5 hrs · Surf Town + Redwoods + Verve Coffee
West Cliff Drive walk past Steamer Lane surf spot. Old boardwalk (open April weekends). Verve Coffee — one of the great California roasters. Henry Cowell Redwoods State Park 15 min inland — cathedral groves, river swimming. A town with great independent restaurants and beach culture completely different from SF.
Monterey Bay — Wildlife Capital
Monterey Bay Aquarium ⭐
2 hrs · Book Tickets · montereybayaquarium.org
One of the world's great aquariums, literally sitting above the bay — you can hear waves through the floor vents. Open Sea tank (1 million gallons): tuna, sunfish, hammerhead sharks. Sea otter exhibit with rescued otters floating on their backs cracking clams. The kelp forest tank is 3 stories tall. Worth the drive on its own.
Elkhorn Slough + Kayaking
Moss Landing · 20 min north of Monterey
The sea otter nursery — kayak the channels eye-level with otters nursing pups, 400+ sea lions on the public dock. Elkhorn Slough Safari and Monterey Bay Kayaks both offer tours. The most accessible extraordinary wildlife encounter in California.
Carmel + Point Lobos
2.5 hrs · No Chain Stores · Best Coastal Reserve
Carmel is a storybook town — no street numbers, no chains, gallery-lined streets, white sand beach. Point Lobos State Reserve (10 min south) is the crown jewel of the California coast: sea otters, harbor seals, sea lions, cormorants all visible from cliff trails over turquoise coves.
Sonoma Coast — Underrated Wildness
Bodega Head + Bodega Bay
1.5 hrs north of SF · Best Whale Watching + Seals
Bodega Head is the best whale-watching headland in Northern California in April. Harbor seals at Doran Beach. Spud Point Crab Co in town — fish and chips eaten in the parking lot, that's the move. Hitchcock filmed The Birds here.
Goat Rock Beach
1.5 hrs north · Harbor Seal Colony · Russian River Mouth
Where the Russian River meets the Pacific. A harbor seal colony on the sandbar, with spring pups. Beach is dramatically split: river on one side, crashing Pacific on the other. Very few tourists come here. One of the most beautiful and underrated spots on the entire California coast.
Salt Point State Park
2 hrs north · Wild Coast · Best Tide Pools
The most dramatic coastal scenery in Sonoma County — sea stacks, kelp forests visible through clear water, bluff trails. April mushroom hunting in the Bishop pines inland. Almost no one comes here. The full wild California coast experience without crowds.
🌴 Farthest South — SLO & Pismo Beach
Big Sur — The Drive Itself
3 hrs from SF · Drive in Daylight
The 90-mile Hwy 1 stretch between Carmel and San Simeon is the most dramatic coastal drive in the US. Bixby Bridge (most photographed in California), McWay Falls (waterfall onto a beach in a cove), Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park (redwoods to the beach). Plan 3+ hours for this section — you will stop at every pullout.
San Luis Obispo ⭐
3.5 hrs from SF · Happiest City in America
SLO has ranked as the happiest city in America repeatedly. Cal Poly university town, beautiful downtown Mission, Bubblegum Alley (70+ years of gum on a narrow alley). Firestone Grill for the oak-smoked tri-tip sandwich — a Central Coast institution. Thursday night farmers market takes over the entire downtown.
Pismo Beach + Oceano Dunes
3.5–4 hrs from SF · Sand Dunes · Beach Town
Pismo is the classic Central Coast beach town — the Oceano Dunes (you can drive vehicles on the sand), wide uncrowded April beach, the Pismo clam history. Come for the enormous sand dunes, the complete gear-shift vibe after a week of SF sophistication.
