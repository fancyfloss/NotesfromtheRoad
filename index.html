🇵🇭 SoMa Pilipinas & the Filipino Bay

The Bay Area has the second-largest Filipino population in the United States. SoMa Pilipinas is an officially designated Filipino cultural district. Daly City — 15 minutes by BART — is known as the most Filipino city outside the Philippines. The food spans turo-turo counters, Filipino-Mexican fusion, elevated tasting menus, and karinderya-style comfort food. You are home. Eat accordingly.

SF Neighborhoods

Every neighborhood in San Francisco is its own city. Different history, different food, different light. These are all of them — with what to do and where to eat once you're there.

Sunniest Microclimate · Immigrant Heart
The Mission

Named after Mission Dolores (1776), SF's oldest building. The Mission was the city's Latin cultural core long before tech arrived — murals on every wall, taquerias on every block.

🍽 Eat
  • La Taqueria — James Beard award, no-rice burrito, the original
  • Tartine Bakery — country sourdough at 5pm, morning bun at 9am
  • El Farolito — open until 2:30am, super burrito al pastor
  • Limon Rotisserie — Peruvian chicken, aji verde, ceviche
  • Alnico — Filipino-American brunch, ube hotcakes, Valencia St
  • Señor Sisig — Filipino-Mexican fusion, sisig burrito
🎯 Do
  • Balmy Alley + Clarion Alley murals — best outdoor art in the US
  • Dolores Park — city views, weekend energy, bring a blanket
  • Mission Dolores — original 1776 chapel, free to enter
  • Valencia St shops, Bi-Rite Creamery, Dandelion Chocolate
🌱 Sat Market: 22nd & Alabama (Noe Valley adjacent)
SF's Second Chinatown · True Local
Inner Richmond

A working-class neighborhood that became home to waves of Chinese, Russian, Irish, Vietnamese, and Korean immigrants. Clement Street is the most diverse restaurant corridor in the city.

🍽 Eat
  • Kissaten HiFi — Japanese & Filipino matcha + coffee, vinyl hi-fi sound, 189 6th Ave
  • Arsicault Bakery — America's best croissant, go before 10am
  • Ton Kiang — gold standard Hakka dim sum, arrive early weekends
  • PPQ Dungeness Island — Vietnamese crab + garlic noodles
  • Turtle Tower — northern Vietnamese pho, clear and fragrant
  • Green Apple Books — legendary SF indie bookshop (not food but essential)
🎯 Do
  • Clement St food crawl — Vietnamese, Chinese, Russian, Korean all on one strip
  • Golden Gate Park north entrance — Dutch Windmills, Buffalo Paddock
  • Lands End trailhead nearby — cliff walk above the Pacific
🌱 Sun Market: Clement St between 2nd–4th Ave
Fog · Surf · The Avenues
Outer Sunset

Once sand dunes, the Sunset was developed in the early 20th century into a quiet residential grid. It became SF's Asian immigrant neighborhood — Chinese and Vietnamese families, surf culture, fog, and a world that doesn't perform for anyone.

🍽 Eat
  • Hook Fish Co. — 4542 Irving St, surf-to-table fish burritos & tacos, daily catch on the board
  • San Tung — legendary dry-fried chicken wings, Irving St
  • Outerlands — wood-fired California, best brunch in the Avenues
  • Devil's Teeth Baking Co. — biscuit sandwiches near Ocean Beach
  • Thanh Long — roasted Dungeness crab since 1971, garlic noodles
  • Polly Ann Ice Cream — taro, ube, lychee, durian, spin the flavor wheel
🎯 Do
  • Ocean Beach — fire pits at sunset, surfers year-round
  • Sutro Baths ruins — free, beautiful, hit at low tide
  • Dutch Windmills at Golden Gate Park — worth the walk
🌱 No dedicated market — shop the Chinese produce shops on Irving
Beat Poets · Italian Espresso · Coit Tower
North Beach

Italian immigrant neighborhood turned Beat Generation capital. Kerouac and Ginsberg wrote here in the 1950s. City Lights Books is still open. The neighborhood smells like espresso and has since 1950.

🍽 Eat
  • Café Trieste — SF's first espresso café since 1956, staff may sing opera
  • Molinari Deli — Italian deli since 1896, Columbus Ave
  • Original Joe's — open until 3am, the Anthony Bourdain Special
  • Sam's Diner — late-night cheeseburger and beer, North Beach institution
  • Vesuvio Bar — Beat generation bar, Kerouac drank here
🎯 Do
  • City Lights Bookstore — still independent, still radical
  • Coit Tower + Filbert Steps — 360° city views, wild parrots on the steps
  • Ross Alley Fortune Cookie Factory — hand-made, buy adult fortunes
🌱 Washington Square: informal Sat morning tai chi
Victorian Mansions · Japan Center
Pac Heights & Japantown

Pacific Heights is SF's wealthiest residential neighborhood — Victorian and Edwardian mansions on streets with bay views. Japantown next door is one of only three surviving Japantowns in the US, rebuilt by the community after WWII internment.

🍽 Eat
  • b. Patisserie — 2821 California St, kouign-amann, croissants that crackle
  • Benkyodo — mochi since 1906, survived the earthquake AND internment
  • Waraku Ramen — honest tonkotsu, Japan Center
  • Sweet Maple — Fillmore St brunch, brioche French toast
  • Japan Center Food Court — rotating ramen, curry, udon, boba
🎯 Do
  • Lyon Street Steps (220 steps) — best free panoramic view in SF
  • Kinokuniya Bookstore — Japanese books, manga, stationery heaven
  • Cherry Blossom Festival (April) — Japantown's biggest annual celebration
  • Fillmore St architecture walk — most intact Victorian block in SF
🌱 Sat Market: Fillmore between Clay & Jackson (seasonal)
Summer of Love · Vintage Shops
Haight-Ashbury

Ground zero for the 1967 Summer of Love and the counterculture movement. Grateful Dead lived here. Janis Joplin lived on Lyon St. The Victorian rowhouses are unchanged. The head shops are still there. The vibe has mellowed but the history hasn't.

🍽 Eat
  • Cha Cha Cha — tapas, sangria, Caribbean-Spanish energy
  • Magnolia Brewing — SF craft beer, excellent food, Victorian pub
  • Pork Store Café — Haight institution, massive brunch plates
  • Escape from New York Pizza — thin-crust slices since 1987
🎯 Do
  • Amoeba Records — the greatest record store in the US, 24,000 sq ft
  • Buena Vista Park — the oldest park in SF, redwoods inside the city
  • Haight & Ashbury corner photo (obligatory)
  • Cole Valley right off Haight — the quiet, neighborhood version
🌱 Cole Valley Farmers Market: Carl & Cole, Sat mornings
LGBTQ+ Capital of the World
The Castro

Harvey Milk's neighborhood, the birthplace of the modern LGBTQ+ rights movement. Milk opened his camera shop on Castro St in 1972 and became the first openly gay person elected to major political office in California. His presence is on every corner.

🍽 Eat
  • Poesia — Italian-Californian, Castro's neighborhood restaurant
  • Orphan Andy's — 24-hour diner, old school counter service
  • Kitchen Story — Asian-California fusion brunch, Castro St
  • Starbelly — California wood-fired, neighborhood staple
🎯 Do
  • Castro Theatre — working 1922 movie palace, Wurlitzer organ before films
  • Harvey Milk Plaza — rainbow flag, memorial
  • Human Rights Campaign Action Center — Harvey Milk archive
  • Corona Heights park — 360° city views, no tourists
🌱 Castro Farmers Market: Noe & Market, Sat mornings
Filipino Cultural District · SFMOMA
SoMa & SoMa Pilipinas

South of Market was SF's industrial warehouse district, then its dot-com epicenter, now its arts and culture hub. SoMa Pilipinas is the officially designated Filipino cultural district — one of the most significant cultural designations in SF history.

🍽 Eat + Drink
  • Mestiza — plant-forward Filipino, SoMa, modern diaspora cooking
  • 4505 Burgers & BBQ — Texas BBQ, Presidential Platter, Divisadero
  • Sightglass Coffee — best third-wave roaster in SF, SoMa warehouse
  • Smuggler's Cove — 550+ rums, tiki bar, fire bowl cocktails
  • Nopa — wood-fired California, open until midnight
🎯 Do
  • SFMOMA — world-class modern art (free Thu 5–9pm)
  • Filipino Cultural Heritage District murals — community history on walls
  • Museum Row — SFMOMA, Contemporary Jewish, California Historical Society
🌱 No dedicated market — Ferry Building 10 min walk
Sunny Village · Saturday Market
Noe Valley

Tucked between Twin Peaks and the Mission, Noe Valley gets more sun than most of SF and has the quiet feeling of a small town that happens to be inside a major city. Stroller capital of SF.

🍽 Eat
  • Contigo — Spanish small plates, excellent pintxos
  • Forage SF — local, seasonal, genuinely neighborhood lunch
  • La Corneta — fresh Mission-style burritos, neighborhood gem
  • Omnivore Books — food bookshop (not food, but essential)
🎯 Do
  • 24th St Saturday Farmers Market — the most local market in SF
  • Twin Peaks hike — accessible from Noe, 360° views
  • Explore the independent boutiques on 24th St
🌱 24th & Sanchez — Sat 8am–1pm, all-year neighborhood market
Boutique · Natural Wine · Arts
Hayes Valley

Built on the site of a freeway that was torn down after the 1989 earthquake. What was once a blight became one of SF's most creative, walkable corridors in the 1990s and 2000s. Still independent, still interesting.

🍽 Eat
  • Soba Ichi — hand-cut buckwheat soba, one of SF's best Japanese
  • Nopa — wood-fired California modern, open very late
  • Rich Table — sardine chips, porcini doughnuts, inventive California
  • Sightglass Coffee — SoMa location nearby
🎯 Do
  • Patricia's Green — neighborhood park, public art, local hangout
  • Absinthe Brasserie for pre-show drinks
  • SF Symphony / War Memorial Opera House nearby
  • Independent clothing boutiques on Hayes St
🌱 Sat: Patricia's Green informal vendors + Fell St market nearby
Ferry Building · Bay Views
Embarcadero & FiDi

The waterfront stretch from the Bay Bridge to Fisherman's Wharf was a freeway until 1989, when the earthquake brought it down. The city had the wisdom not to rebuild it — the result is the most beautiful waterfront boulevard in the US.

🍽 Eat
  • Ferry Building Marketplace — Hog Island oysters, Blue Bottle, Cowgirl Creamery
  • Hog Island Oyster Co. — raw bar at the Ferry Building counter
  • Waterbar — aquarium tanks, bay views, Pacific oyster selection
  • Red's Java House — Pier 30 shack, cheeseburger on sourdough since 1955
  • Pier 23 Café — local waterfront bar, live music weekends
  • Crustacean — garlic noodles invented here, Dungeness crab
🎯 Do
  • Ferry Building Farmers Market — Sat/Tue/Thu
  • Bay Bridge views from Pier 14 at sunset — best free view on the waterfront
  • Exploratorium at Pier 15 — adult science museum, genuinely mind-bending
🌱 Ferry Building: Sat 8am–2pm · Tue 10am–2pm · Thu 10am–2pm
Oldest Chinatown in America
Chinatown

The oldest Chinatown in North America, established in 1848 by Chinese immigrants who arrived for the Gold Rush. Burned down in 1906, rebuilt immediately by the community. 24 blocks, 100,000 people, the highest density of residents in SF.

🍽 Eat
  • Golden Gate Bakery — THE egg tart, 45-min wait, completely worth it
  • Good Mong Kok — $1-2 dim sum pastries, line of grandmothers at 8am
  • R&G Lounge — salt-and-pepper crab, Cantonese seafood banquet
  • Sam Wo Restaurant — since 1907, jook and wonton noodles, cash only
  • Li Po Lounge — dark, lantern-lit, Chinese mai tais, since 1937
🎯 Do
  • Ross Alley Fortune Cookie Factory — hand-pressed, free sample
  • Waverly Place — painted balconies, temple interiors
  • Chinese New Year parade route (Feb/March) — biggest in the world outside Asia
🌱 Stockton St produce shops open daily — best prices in the city
GGB Views · Crissy Field
Marina & Cow Hollow

Built on landfill from the 1915 Panama-Pacific International Exposition. The neighborhood liquefied in the 1989 earthquake (it was built on fill and rubble). Rebuilt and now one of SF's most picturesque neighborhoods, with views of the Golden Gate from almost every street.

🍽 Eat
  • Atelier Crenn — 3 Michelin stars, poetic cuisine, special occasion
  • Pizzeria Delfina — SF's best Neapolitan pizza
  • A16 — Southern Italian, excellent wine program
  • Tacolicious — Marina's neighborhood taco spot
  • Réveille Coffee — best coffee in the Marina
🎯 Do
  • Crissy Field tidal marsh walk — Golden Gate views the whole way
  • Fort Mason Center for Arts & Culture — galleries, music, food events
  • Wave Organ — acoustic sea sculpture made from marble and pipes
  • Palace of Fine Arts — domed rotunda, ducks, romantic at sunset
🌱 Fort Mason Center: Sun 9am–2pm, Saturday market seasonally
Real SF · Filipino Heart · Under the Radar
Excelsior & Outer Mission

The most ethnically diverse neighborhood in SF, often overlooked by visitors. A working-class community of Filipino, Latino, Chinese, and Irish families. No tourists. Real food. The SF that tourists never find.

🍽 Eat
  • Kusina Ni Tess — Filipino homestyle, adobo, tapsilog, under $15
  • Turo Café & Grill — turo-turo Filipino counter, neighborhood staple
  • Super Star Restaurant — Filipino-Chinese lunch plates, Excelsior
  • Fili-Taste Grill — affordable Filipino standbys, neighborhood gem
  • La Lengua — Mexican-Californian, best value in the Outer Mission
🎯 Do
  • McLaren Park — often overlooked 317-acre park with city views
  • Filipino community murals on Mission St (Outer Mission stretch)
🌱 Ocean Ave Farmers Market: Thursdays, year-round
Immigrant Kitchens · Affordable Gems
The Tenderloin

The Tenderloin is SF's most densely populated neighborhood and its most complicated — a dense mix of SRO hotels, community organizations, Vietnamese and Thai immigrant kitchens, and one of the best concentrations of affordable ethnic food in the entire city.

🍽 Eat
  • Saigon Sandwich — best bánh mì in SF, under $6, always a line
  • Hai Ky Mi Gia — Teochew Chinese-Vietnamese noodles, order dry
  • Turtle Tower — northern-style pho, the best broth in the city
  • Bourbon & Branch — actual speakeasy, password required, book ahead
  • Mensho Tokyo — Japanese ramen imported from Tokyo, exceptional
🎯 Do
  • The Tenderloin Museum — the neighborhood's own history, worth a visit
  • Glide Memorial Church — legendary community institution
  • Little Saigon on Larkin — best Vietnamese food corridor outside the Richmond
🌱 Boeddeker Park Farmers Market: Thursdays (seasonal)
Hilltop Views · Off-Grid Energy
Bernal Heights

A hilly neighborhood that has resisted gentrification better than most of SF. Community gardens, artists, longtime Latino families, and the best 360° city view you can access without a car — just walk to the top of Bernal Hill.

🍽 Eat
  • Hillside Supper Club — neighborhood wine bar, seasonal small plates
  • Cellarmaker Brewing — SF craft beer, walk-in, excellent hop-forward IPAs
  • Lolo — Mexican with a wine list, lively Cortland Ave
  • Good Good Culture Club — New American, farm-to-table Bernal
🎯 Do
  • Bernal Hill summit — 360° unobstructed city view, free, no crowds
  • Cortland Ave independent shops and cafes
  • Holly Park — quiet neighborhood park with city glimpses
🌱 Precita Park micro-market: informal Sat mornings

Food by Cuisine

The Bay's food scene runs on immigrant cooking. Every cuisine below has its own tradition, its own community, and its own SF story. Pick your hunger and go deep.

🇵🇭 Filipino — The Bay is Home

From turo-turo counters in Daly City to contemporary tasting menus in the Kimpton — Filipino food in the Bay spans the full spectrum. SoMa Pilipinas is a designated cultural district. You are home. Eat from the top down and bottom up.

Fisherman's Wharf · Fine Dining
ABACÁ ⭐
Kimpton Alton · restaurantabaca.com
Contemporary Filipino-Californian. Sisig fried rice, crispy chicken skin with spicy vinegar, ube colada, calamansi daiquiri. Panaderia Abacá does ensaymada and bibingka all day. Book ahead.
Union Square · Tasting Menu
Ox + Tiger ⭐
Filipino-Japanese Fusion
Where Japanese and Filipino flavors collide in a tasting-menu format. Inventive, seasonal, and deeply personal. One of the most exciting Filipino restaurants in California.
Mission · Valencia St
Alnico
Filipino-American Brunch · 1050 Valencia
Ube hotcakes, niloco mo ako plates, Filipino-American weekend brunch done with love and creativity. Photo-ready, soul-satisfying.
Mission · Filipino-Mexican
Señor Sisig
990 Valencia · Multiple Locations
The Bay Area original — sisig burritos, sisig bowls, sisig fries. Filipino-Mexican mashup that became an institution. Affordable, always fire.
Tenderloin / Excelsior
Kusina Ni Tess
Authentic Homestyle · All Day Filipino Breakfast
Tapsilog, adobo, laing, sinigang — cooked like someone's nanay made it. Under $15 for a full meal. The best value Filipino in the city.
Daly City · BART to Colma
Chibog ⭐
Turo-Turo · Point and Eat · Cash Only
Point at what you want. Sizzling sisig with fried egg, kare-kare, grilled skewers. Daly City is the capital of Filipino America. Make the trip.
Temescal, Oakland
FOB Kitchen
Filipino Brunch · Oakland
Excellent Filipino brunch in Oakland's coolest neighborhood. Silog plates, crispy pork, shrimp chips. Pair with a Temescal neighborhood walk.
Excelsior
Turo Café & Grill
Counter Service · Excelsior SF
Classic turo-turo counter in the Excelsior — the most Filipino neighborhood in SF proper. Lunch plates, rice, viand. Neighborhood institution.
Daly City · Multiple
Red Ribbon + Goldilocks
Filipino Bakery Chains
Ube cake, ensaymada, pan de sal, bibingka. Pick up a whole cake. No arguments.

🇻🇳 Vietnamese — The Tenderloin to the Sunset

Vietnamese refugees arrived after 1975 and built a food culture that shaped the entire city. Little Saigon is in the Tenderloin. The Richmond is full of Vietnamese kitchens. Daly City has its own corridor.

FiDi · 220 California St
Turtle Tower ⭐
Northern Vietnamese Pho · Returned 2025
Northern-style pho — clear, fragrant, deeply savory. No southern-style hoisin overload. The best broth in the city. The bún thang is a revelation.
FiDi · 195 Pine St
Crustacean ⭐
Secret Kitchen · Garlic Noodles Invented Here
Chef Helene An's garlic noodles — invented circa 1978 in a kitchen-within-a-kitchen that only the An family enters. Roasted Dungeness crab. Stunning new FiDi location.
Tenderloin · Under $6
Saigon Sandwich
Best Bánh Mì in SF · Always a Line
Pâté, pork, daikon, jalapeño, cilantro on a perfect baguette. Under $6. One of the great sandwiches in the world at any price. Get two.
Tenderloin · Teochew
Hai Ky Mi Gia
Chinese-Vietnamese Noodles
Thick egg noodles, braised duck or char siu. Order dry (khô) with soup on the side. The cuisine of ethnic Chinese Vietnamese — underknown and extraordinary.
Inner Richmond
Y&Y Vietnamese
Clement St · Bún Bò Huế
The bún bò Huế (spicy Central Vietnamese beef noodle soup) is one of the best in the city. Huge portions, very reasonable prices.
Outer Sunset
Thanh Long
Roasted Dungeness Crab + Garlic Noodles · Since 1971
SF Vietnamese royalty. Garlic noodles and roasted Dungeness crab — a combination so good it became the signature of Vietnamese-Californian cooking.
Chinatown · Since 1912
Swan Oyster Depot ⭐
18 Seats · Cash Only · Mon–Sat 8–2:30pm
Not Chinese, but the most SF counter there is. Half-Sicilian family, same menu since 1912. The crab back and oysters are why Bourdain called it one of his favorite places on earth.
Chinatown
R&G Lounge
Cantonese Seafood · Live Tanks
Salt-and-pepper crab, hand-pulled noodles, sizzling platters. Cantonese cooking at its most festive. A proper Chinese banquet in the heart of Chinatown.
Richmond · Weekend Dim Sum
Ton Kiang ⭐
Hakka Dim Sum · Arrive by 10am
SF's gold standard for dim sum. Hakka-style, meticulous. Turnip cake, cheung fun, char siu bao. Arrive early — waits are serious by 11am.
Sunset
Dragon Beaux
Modern Dim Sum · Rainbow Har Gow
Contemporary dim sum with beautiful presentation. Rainbow har gow, truffle dumplings. Very popular — book for weekend mornings.
Outer Sunset · Irving St
San Tung
Dry-Fried Chicken Wings
The dry-fried wings are legendary — crispy, chewy, addictive. Handmade dumplings also exceptional. Lines before opening. An SF neighborhood institution.
Chinatown · Since 1907
Sam Wo Restaurant
Oldest Chinese Restaurant in SF
Since 1907. Jook and wonton noodle soup. Cash only, cheap, three floors, zero frills, genuinely good.
Japantown · Ramen
Waraku Ramen
Tonkotsu · Japantown
Honest tonkotsu in Japantown. Chashu pork, jammy egg. Perfect after Benkyodo mochi and Kinokuniya browsing.
Mission · Izakaya
Izakaya Rintaro ⭐
Hand-Built Wood Interior · Robata
Hand-built interior, Japanese whisky, robata grill small plates. Housemade tofu, in-house pickles, exceptional yakitori. One of the best Japanese restaurants in the Bay.
Hayes Valley · Counter
Sebo
Seasonal Japanese Fish Counter
Serious seasonal fish sourced with obsessive care. Counter seating, omakase energy. The place chefs eat on their days off.
Palo Alto · Near Stanford
Ramen Nagi
Tokyo Chain · Stanford Shopping Center
The legendary Tokyo ramen chain. Black King (squid ink tonkotsu) and Red King (spicy) are the orders. Line every time — worth it.
Japantown · Since 1906
Benkyodo
Oldest Japanese-American Confectionery in US
Hand-rolled mochi and manju made from 120-year-old family recipes. Survived the earthquake. Survived internment. Still here. Buy a box.
Nob Hill · Polk St
To Hyang
Homestyle Korean · Bourdain + Cosentino Ate Here
Banchan from the back shed, fish head curry, ginseng soju, pork belly with date soju. The homestyle Korean that changed how SF sees Korean cooking.
Inner Sunset
Namu Gaji
Korean-American · Seasonal
Korean flavors meeting California produce. Dduk bok ki, bibimbap, seasonal banchan. The most inventive Korean cooking in SF.
Japantown Area
Ju-Ni
Omakase Sushi · Technically Japanese-Korean
SF's best value omakase — Korean-American chef doing extraordinary nigiri in a 12-seat counter. Book ahead, very hard to get into.

🍛 Indian South Bay — Sunnyvale, Fremont, Milpitas

The highest concentration of South Asian food outside New Jersey. Dosas taller than your arm. Hyderabadi biryani cooked properly. Mumbai chaat. 45 min south on Caltrain or 101. Completely worth the drive.

Sunnyvale · South Indian
Dosa by Dosa ⭐
Masala Dosa · Mumbai Street Chaat
The masala dosa (fermented rice crepe, potato filling) with three chutneys and sambhar. Pani puri, bhel puri, dahi papdi. Under $12. Always packed with Indian families — the highest endorsement.
Mountain View
Sakoon
Modern Indian · Weekend Buffet
Most acclaimed upscale Indian in the South Bay. Butter chicken and saag paneer with genuine refinement. Weekend buffet constantly rotating and fresh.
Fremont · Biryani
Dum India Kitchen
Dum Biryani · Fremont's Little India
Slow-cooked biryani in sealed pots, opened tableside with a puff of saffron steam. Fremont Blvd is an entire Indian food world — walk it before or after eating.
Milpitas
Chaat Bhavan
Indian Street Chaat
Pani puri, sev puri, bhel puri, dahi puri. The truest form of Mumbai street food in the Bay. Plastic tables, extraordinary food, $5–10 plates.
Fremont
Udupi Palace
South Indian Vegetarian Thali
All-vegetarian South Indian thali — steel bowls of dal, sabzi, sambhar, rasam, rotating until you stop. Cheap, warm, a window into vegetarian Indian cooking at its finest.
Mission · James Beard Award
La Taqueria ⭐
The OG Mission Burrito · No Rice · 2889 Mission
The James Beard America's Classic. Carne asada or carnitas, beans, cheese, salsa — no rice, no filler. A philosophical position on burritos. Daily lines. This is the ur-burrito.
Mission · Since 1953
La Palma Mexicatessen
Tortilla Factory · Hot Off the Press
Masa and tortillas made fresh all day. Hot off the press at $1 each. Pupusas, tamales, handmade tortillas with carnitas. SF's oldest Mexican institution.
Mission · Late Night
El Farolito
Open Until 2:30am · Super Burrito
Open until 2:30am. Burritos the size of your forearm. Al pastor at midnight is a spiritual experience. The Mission's late-night warrior since 1966.
Mission · Peruvian
Limon Rotisserie
Peruvian Chicken · Ceviche
Rotisserie chicken with aji amarillo, ceviche, causa. One of the great cheap meals in SF — the chicken and green sauce alone is worth the trip.
Mission · Salvadoran
El Zocalo
Pupusas · Most Underrated in the Mission
Pupusas stuffed with cheese, loroco, or chicharron. Under $4 each. Curtido and salsa. The most underrated cuisine in the Mission.
Oakland · Fruitvale
Fruitvale Village Taquerias
International Blvd · Most Authentic in Bay Area
Oakland's Fruitvale is the most authentic Mexican food corridor in the whole Bay. Weekend birria, carnitas, Oaxacan mole. $3 tacos. Bourdain stopped here. Walk the strip and follow your nose.
Oakland · Caribbean
Miss Ollie's
901 Washington · Afro-Caribbean Soul Food
Bourdain and Bobby Seale (Black Panther co-founder) had fried chicken, rice and peas, collard greens here. Required Oakland stop to understand the city's soul.

🇧🇷 Brazilian Flavor in the Bay

The Bay Area has a vibrant Brazilian community — particularly in the South Bay and East Bay. From churrascarias to açaí to pão de queijo — the Brazilian presence is real and delicious.

Burlingame / South Bay
Espetus Churrascaria
Brazilian Rodizio · All-You-Can-Eat
The full rodizio experience — passadores bring endless cuts of grilled meat tableside (picanha, fraldinha, linguiça, chicken hearts) until you flip your card to red. The salad bar alone is worth the trip. Classic SF South American gathering spot.
San Francisco · Various
Açaí Republic
Brazilian Açaí Bowls
Authentic açaí bowls the way Brazilians actually make them — thick, not sweet, topped with granola and banana. Not the Instagram version. The real thing.
East Bay / Online
Pão de Queijo & Brazilian Bakeries
Brazilian Cheese Bread · Community Bakers
Look for Brazilian community pop-ups and bakeries in the East Bay — the pão de queijo (warm, chewy cheese bread) and coxinha (chicken croquettes) are the things to find.
Mission / Inner Sunset
The Swirl
Brazilian-Inspired Caipirinha Bar
Caipirinhas made with real cachaça, not substitutions. Brazilian bar snacks. The right soundtrack. SF's best representation of the Rio nightlife sensibility.
Polk St · Cash Only · 8–2:30 Mon–Sat

No marketing budget. No Instagram strategy. No reservation system. Just food made by people who've been doing it the same way for decades.

Bourdain filmed in San Francisco four times across all his shows. Every time he did the same thing: skipped the trendy spots, went for the counter with 18 seats and a 100-year menu. The places below are in part a map of where he led — quietly.

Wildlife & Hiking

The Bay Area has more accessible wildlife in more variety than almost anywhere else in the US. Sea otters, elephant seals, gray whales, harbor seals — all within a two-hour drive. And the hiking is extraordinary.

🦭 Wildlife You Can Actually See
Year-Round · Free · Pier 39
Sea Lions at Pier 39
Up to 900 hauling out · K-Dock
California sea lions colonized Pier 39's K-dock after the 1989 earthquake and never left. Up to 900 haul out, bark, and sunbathe. Marine Mammal Center volunteers on-site. Best before 10am when the tourists haven't arrived yet.
🦦Moss Landing · 1.5 hrs
Sea Otters at Elkhorn Slough
Kayak Tour · elkhornsloughsafari.com
Highest concentration of sea otters in California. Spring: mothers float on their backs with pups cracking shellfish on their chests while nursing. Kayak tours put you eye-level with the otters. Book morning pontoon. Extraordinary.
🚣 Book: elkhornsloughsafari.com or Monterey Bay Kayaks
Point Reyes · 1.5 hrs
Elephant Seals at Chimney Rock
Free Cliff Viewpoint · Point Reyes NS
Hundreds of elephant seals below — viewed from a cliff trail. Point Reyes also has tule elk on Tomales Point, harbor seals at Drakes Beach, and gray whales from the lighthouse. One of the most biodiverse coastal parks in the US.
1.5 hrs South · Self-Guided April
Elephant Seals at Año Nuevo
Walk Among Them · reserveamerica.com
Walk among hundreds of Northern elephant seals on an open beach. Males weigh up to 2,000 lbs. April pups learning to swim in the shallows. One of the most extraordinary close-contact wildlife experiences in North America.
Bodega Head · April Peak
Gray Whale Migration
Bodega Head · Point Reyes · Sonoma Coast
April is peak northbound migration — up to 26,000 gray whales heading from Baja to Alaska. Best land viewing: Bodega Head (Sonoma), Chimney Rock (Point Reyes), Marin Headlands. Look for grey spout and rounded back rolling through the swell. Bring binoculars.
Monterey · 2 hrs
Monterey Bay Wildlife
Kayak · Aquarium · Hopkins Reef
Kayak from Cannery Row through kelp forests with sea otters alongside. The Monterey Bay Aquarium deck looks directly over the bay — waves come through the floor vents. Hopkins Marine Refuge has harbor seals nursing pups yards from downtown Monterey.
🦦 Otters visible 50 yards from the kayak launch — montereybayaquarium.org
🥾 Best Hikes — Marin, SF, Point Reyes, Sonoma
SF · 2.2 miles · Easy
Land's End Trail
Sutro Baths → Eagles Point
The best urban hike in SF. Sea cliffs, hidden coves, ruins of Sutro Baths, ruins of a Greek temple in the cypress forest. Golden Gate views at every turn. April wildflowers. Free. Dramatic. Don't skip it.
Marin · 4.7 miles · Moderate
Hawk Hill + Rodeo Beach
Golden Gate NRA · Headlands
Drive over the GGB, exit right. Ridge walk with the bridge below. Hawk Hill: April wildflowers, golden eagles. Descend to Rodeo Beach — colored serpentine pebbles, harbor seals at Rodeo Lagoon. The definitive Marin Headlands hike.
Marin · 3.5 miles · Easy
Tennessee Valley
Marin Headlands → Secret Pacific Cove
Flat valley through Marin Headlands grassland to a completely isolated Pacific cove. April: lupine and poppies, red-tailed hawks, harbor seals occasionally. 20 minutes from the Golden Gate. No crowds on weekday mornings.
Marin · 6.2 miles · Moderate-Hard
Mount Tamalpais — Matt Davis Trail
Stinson Beach Loop · Bay Views
The classic Mount Tam traverse: redwood forest, chaparral ridges, Bay views across the entire region. Connects to Muir Woods at the bottom. April: flowers everywhere, streams full. Swim at Stinson Beach after.
Marin · 2.4 miles · Book Entry
Muir Woods Cathedral Grove
Old-Growth Redwoods · recreation.gov
Trees up to 1,000 years old, 250 feet tall. A cathedral of silence. Book timed entry at recreation.gov. Weekday mornings: you may have the grove entirely to yourself.
Sonoma · Underrated
Sonoma Coast — Goat Rock + Salt Point
Harbor Seals · Wild Coast · Almost No Tourists
Goat Rock Beach: harbor seal colony at the Russian River mouth, pups in spring. Salt Point State Park: sea stacks, kelp forests visible through clear water, bluff trails. The most dramatic coastal scenery in Sonoma County and almost no one goes here.

The Coastal Drive

Highway 1 south from SF to Pismo Beach is one of the great road trips on earth. Drive it in pieces or do the whole thing. Every pullout has a view worth stopping for — drive south in the morning for the best light.

🚗 SF → Pacifica → Half Moon Bay → Santa Cruz → Monterey → Big Sur → SLO/Pismo

Total drive time SF to Pismo Beach: ~4 hours without stops. You will stop constantly. Perfect as separate day trips or one overnight coastal run. The Big Sur section between Carmel and San Simeon is the most dramatic — drive it in daylight.

Pacifica to Half Moon Bay — 20–45 min from SF
Pacifica State Beach
20 min · First Beach South of SF · Surfers
Wide, wild, often uncrowded. Surfers year-round. Sea cliff trails above the beach with dramatic views. Pacifica taco trucks on Palmetto Ave are the secret food find on this stretch.
Sam's Chowder House ⭐
Half Moon Bay · 4210 N Cabrillo Hwy · Oceanfront
On an oceanside bluff directly above the Pacific. Lobster roll (Today Show "best in America"), award-winning clam chowder, fire pits, Adirondack chairs. Dogs welcome. Book weekends ahead.
Half Moon Bay State Beaches + Mavericks
45 min · 4 Miles of Sand · Whale Watching
Four connected state beaches. April gray whale migration makes this excellent for land-based whale watching. Saturday farmers market in town. Mavericks big-wave surf break viewable from shore in winter.
Pescadero to Santa Cruz
Pescadero & Duarte's Tavern
1 hr · Since 1894 · Artichoke Country
A farming village tourists don't find. Duarte's has served artichoke soup and olallieberry pie since 1894. Pescadero Marsh bird sanctuary. Pigeon Point Lighthouse nearby.
Año Nuevo State Park
1.5 hrs · Walk Among Elephant Seals
Walk among hundreds of elephant seals in April. Males up to 2,000 lbs, pups in the shallows. Combine with Duarte's lunch on the same drive. reserveamerica.com
Santa Cruz
1.5 hrs · Surf Town · Redwoods · Verve Coffee
West Cliff Drive past Steamer Lane surf spot. Verve Coffee — one of the great California roasters. Henry Cowell Redwoods 15 min inland for cathedral groves and river swimming.
Monterey — Wildlife Capital of California
Monterey Bay Aquarium ⭐
2 hrs · montereybayaquarium.org · Book Ahead
One of the world's great aquariums sitting directly above the bay. Sea otter exhibit, 3-story kelp forest tank, million-gallon Open Sea tank with hammerheads. Waves come through the floor vents. Worth the drive on its own.
Elkhorn Slough Kayaking
Moss Landing · Sea Otter Nursery
Highest concentration of sea otters in California — kayak eye-level with otters nursing pups, 400+ sea lions on the dock. elkhornsloughsafari.com for tours.
Carmel + Point Lobos
2.5 hrs · No Chain Stores · Best Coastal Reserve
Storybook town with no chains, white sand beach, gallery-lined streets. Point Lobos State Reserve (10 min south): sea otters, harbor seals, sea lions, turquoise coves. Crown jewel of the California coast.
Sonoma Coast (North) — The Underrated Direction
Bodega Head + Bodega Bay
1.5 hrs North · Best Whale Watching
Best whale-watching headland in Northern California in April. Harbor seals at Doran Beach. Spud Point Crab Co — fish and chips in the parking lot. Hitchcock filmed The Birds here.
Goat Rock Beach + Duncans Mills
1.5 hrs North · Harbor Seals · Russian River
Where the Russian River meets the Pacific. Harbor seal colony with spring pups. Duncans Mills nearby — tiny gold rush town, antiques, complete silence, the Bay Area's best kept secret.
🌴 Farthest South — SLO & Pismo
Big Sur
3 hrs from SF · Drive in Daylight Only
90 miles of the most dramatic coastal road in America. Bixby Bridge, McWay Falls (waterfall onto a private beach), Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park. Nepenthe restaurant for a cliff-edge burger. Stop at every pullout.
San Luis Obispo ⭐
3.5 hrs · Downtown + Thursday Night Market
SLO has ranked as the happiest city in America repeatedly. Beautiful mission downtown, Thursday night farmers market shuts down Higuera St entirely. Firestone Grill for the oak-smoked tri-tip sandwich. One night here is the trip within the trip.
Sam's Anchor Cafe — Tiburon
Marin · Ferry from SF · Bay Institution
Not on Hwy 1 but the water counterpart — take the ferry from the Ferry Building to Tiburon. Sam's Anchor Cafe since 1920: fresh seafood, views of Angel Island, Alcatraz, and the SF skyline from the waterfront deck. Dog-friendly, live music weekends. Bay institution.
Malibu Farm Tiburon
Tiburon · 9 Main St · Farm-to-Table Views
Helene Henderson's farm-to-table California cooking — vegetable-forward, locally sourced — with a stunning SF skyline backdrop. Pair with Sam's for a full Tiburon afternoon.
Pismo Beach + Oceano Dunes
4 hrs from SF · Sand Dunes · Beach Town
Classic Central Coast beach town. Oceano Dunes you can drive on. Wide uncrowded beach. The complete gear-shift after a week of SF sophistication.

Day Trips

By drive time from SF. All doable in a day. Some deserve two nights.

20–40 Minutes
20 min
Marin Headlands
Hawk Hill · Battery Spencer · Rodeo Beach
Cross the Golden Gate, exit right. Most dramatic GGB views from any direction. April wildflowers, sea birds, harbor seals at Rodeo Lagoon. No tourist infrastructure once you leave the bridge. Free.

Ferry from Ferry Building · Sam's + Malibu Farm
Take the Ferry Building ferry — the best 30-minute boat ride in California. Sausalito: houseboats, best skyline view from land. Tiburon: Sam's Anchor Cafe and Malibu Farm both on Main St. Combine both towns in one day.

Old-Growth Redwoods · recreation.gov
Timed entry required. Cathedral Grove loop 2.4 miles. After: Muir Beach, lunch at the Pelican Inn (an actual English pub in a Marin meadow). Sol Food in Mill Valley for Puerto Rican food en route back.
45 min
Half Moon Bay
Sam's Chowder · Beaches · Whale Watching
Sam's Chowder House for lunch on the bluff. April gray whale watching from state beaches. Pescadero artichoke fields en route. Saturday farmers market. Mavericks viewable from shore.
1 hr
Point Reyes National Seashore
Wildlife · Lighthouse · Tomales Bay Oysters
April: elephant seals at Chimney Rock, tule elk on Tomales Point, gray whales from the lighthouse trail. Hog Island Oyster Farm in Marshall (Fri–Mon, book ahead). Inverness Bakery for morning pastry. A full day.
1 hr
Sonoma — Wine + Coast
Hog Island + Bodega Head + Armstrong Redwoods
Costeaux Bakery Healdsburg for breakfast. Armstrong Redwoods (free, spectacular). Hog Island Marshall for oysters (Fri–Mon, book ahead). Balletto Vineyards walk-in. Fern Bar Sebastopol for dinner.
2+ Hours — Overnight Worthy
2 hrs
Monterey + Carmel
Aquarium · Sea Otters · Point Lobos
Full day: Aquarium morning, kayak with sea otters, Fisherman's Wharf lunch, Carmel afternoon, Point Lobos at sunset. One night in Carmel makes the Big Sur drive next morning the perfect conclusion.
3.5–4 hrs · Overnight Best
San Luis Obispo + Pismo Beach
Happiest City + Dunes + Thursday Market
SLO Thursday night farmers market is one of California's best. Firestone Grill tri-tip. Pismo's Oceano Dunes. One night in SLO makes this the trip within the trip.
